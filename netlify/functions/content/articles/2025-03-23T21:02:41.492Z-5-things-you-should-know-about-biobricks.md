## What are BioBricks and How Do They Work?

BioBricks are standardized, interchangeable genetic parts that can be combined to create new biological systems.  Think of them as the LEGOs of biology.  Each BioBrick is a DNA sequence with precisely defined input and output functions, ensuring predictable behavior when combined with other parts. This standardization is crucial; it allows researchers to easily assemble, test, and share genetic circuits, accelerating the pace of biological innovation. ![Microscopic image of BioBricks](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1451187580459-43490279c0fa?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

These genetic parts typically encode for specific biological functions, such as promoting gene expression, producing a specific protein, or responding to environmental stimuli. They come in a variety of types, including promoters (which control gene expression), ribosome binding sites (which initiate protein synthesis), and coding sequences (which specify the amino acid sequence of a protein).
