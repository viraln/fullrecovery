## 3. Fostering "Sustainable AI" Through Algorithmic and Data Efficiency

The computational demands of modern AI, especially deep learning, contribute significantly to the "Environmental Impact of AI". Training large language models (LLMs) and complex neural networks requires massive amounts of energy, leading to a substantial "Reducing AI Carbon Footprint" challenge. However, advancements in algorithmic efficiency and data optimization are paving the way for "Sustainable AI".

Here's how:

*   **Knowledge Distillation:** Training smaller, more efficient models that mimic the behavior of larger models.
*   **Pruning:** Removing unnecessary connections in neural networks to reduce their size and computational complexity.
*   **Quantization:** Reducing the precision of numerical representations in neural networks to decrease memory usage and energy consumption.
*   **Federated Learning:** Training models on decentralized data sources without transferring the data to a central server, reducing data storage and transmission costs.
*   **Active Learning:** Selecting the most informative data points for training, reducing the amount of data required to achieve a desired level of accuracy.
