## Understanding the Foundations: What is Post-Humanism?

At its core, post-humanism is a philosophical and cultural movement that questions the traditional definition of "human." It challenges the limitations of the human condition, including mortality, disease, and cognitive constraints, and explores the potential for overcoming them through technological advancements. It's important to distinguish post-humanism from *transhumanism*. While both are related, transhumanism is more focused on the practical application of technology to enhance human capabilities, whereas post-humanism encompasses a broader critique of humanism and its assumptions.

Think of it this way: Humanism celebrates human potential within its natural boundaries. Transhumanism actively seeks to *transcend* those boundaries. Post-humanism questions the very definition of those boundaries and what it means to be human in the first place.
