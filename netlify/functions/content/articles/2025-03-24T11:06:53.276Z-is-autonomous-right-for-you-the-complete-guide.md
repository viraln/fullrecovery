## What is Autonomous?  Understanding the Spectrum of Automation

The term "autonomous" refers to systems capable of operating independently, without direct human control.  This encompasses a broad spectrum, from fully autonomous vehicles navigating complex urban environments to robotic vacuum cleaners performing simple tasks.  The level of autonomy varies significantly, often categorized using levels from 0 (no automation) to 5 (full automation).

* Level 0: No automation. The driver is in complete control.
* Level 1: Driver-assistance systems (e.g., adaptive cruise control).
* Level 2: Partial automation (e.g., lane keeping assist and adaptive cruise control working together).
* Level 3: Conditional automation. The system can handle most driving tasks, but the driver must be ready to take over.
* Level 4: High automation. The system can handle all driving tasks in specific geographical areas.
* Level 5: Full automation. The system can handle all driving tasks in all conditions.
