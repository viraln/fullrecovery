## What is Spatial Computing?

Spatial computing goes beyond simply displaying 3D images; it involves understanding and interacting with the three-dimensional space around us.  It leverages technologies like VR, AR, computer vision, sensor fusion, and artificial intelligence (AI) to create immersive, interactive environments. Unlike traditional computing which relies on 2D interfaces, spatial computing allows users to manipulate digital objects within a real-world context, or within a fully virtual environment, using natural gestures, voice commands, and even eye tracking. ![A graphic showing the difference between traditional computing and spatial computing](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1484807352052-23338990c6c6?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **EXPERT TIP:**  Understanding the difference between AR and VR is crucial. AR overlays digital information onto the real world (think Pokémon Go), while VR creates entirely simulated environments. Spatial computing encompasses both, plus other technologies that blend the physical and digital.
