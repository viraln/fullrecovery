## What is the Decentralized Web (dWeb)?

The Decentralized Web, often contrasted with the centralized web we know today, is a vision of the internet built on principles of decentralization, transparency, and user control. Unlike the centralized web dominated by a handful of powerful corporations controlling data, servers, and algorithms, the dWeb distributes power and control among its users.  This is achieved through technologies like blockchain, peer-to-peer networking, and cryptographic techniques.  Instead of relying on centralized servers owned by large companies, data is spread across a network of independent nodes, making it more resilient, secure, and less susceptible to censorship or single points of failure. ![A visual representation of a decentralized network compared to a centralized one](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1640032152000-f273e2ca6922?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **DID YOU KNOW?** The concept of a decentralized internet predates the World Wide Web itself.  Early visions of a peer-to-peer network laid the groundwork for the technologies powering the dWeb today.
