## 2. The Rise of Carbon Removal Technology

While reducing emissions is crucial, it's not enough to avert the worst effects of climate change. We also need to actively remove carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. Carbon removal technology, once considered a futuristic fantasy, is rapidly becoming a reality, attracting significant investment and innovation.

![An image depicting a carbon capture facility or a direct air capture machine.](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1502637098811-fa9526d2b659?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

This field encompasses a wide range of approaches:

*   **Direct Air Capture (DAC):** Machines that suck CO2 directly from the air, which can then be stored underground or used to create valuable products.
*   **Bioenergy with Carbon Capture and Storage (BECCS):** Burning biomass for energy and capturing the CO2 emissions, effectively creating a carbon-negative energy source.
*   **Enhanced Weathering:** Spreading crushed rocks, such as basalt, on land or in the ocean to accelerate the natural process of CO2 absorption.
*   **Afforestation and Reforestation:** Planting trees to absorb CO2 from the atmosphere, although the permanence of this carbon sink can be a concern.
*   **Ocean Alkalinity Enhancement:** Adding alkaline materials to the ocean to increase its capacity to absorb CO2.

**Key Trends in Carbon Removal:**

*   **Increased Investment:** Venture capital funding for carbon removal startups has surged in recent years, indicating growing confidence in the potential of these technologies.
*   **Technological Breakthroughs:** Researchers are making significant progress in improving the efficiency and reducing the cost of carbon removal technologies.
*   **Government Support:** Governments are beginning to recognize the importance of carbon removal and are providing funding and incentives for its development and deployment.
