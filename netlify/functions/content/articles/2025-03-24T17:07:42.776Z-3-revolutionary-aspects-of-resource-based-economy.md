The defining characteristic of an RBE is its focus on need-based allocation of resources.  In contrast to market economies where resources are distributed based on purchasing power, an RBE prioritizes equitable distribution based on the needs of the population.  ![A graphic showing equitable resource distribution across a population](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1616259833980-afea7be4732f?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)  This means that access to essential resources like food, shelter, healthcare, and education is guaranteed for everyone, regardless of their income or social status.

This paradigm shift requires a fundamental rethinking of value.  In a market economy, value is determined by market forces; in an RBE, value is determined by human need and environmental sustainability.  This necessitates a shift in social priorities, away from material possessions and towards human well-being and ecological balance.
