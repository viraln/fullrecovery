## 1. Unveiling the Quantum Advantage: What are Quantum Decision Trees?

At its core, a decision tree is a predictive model that maps observations about an item to conclusions about the item's target value. Classical decision trees work by recursively partitioning the data based on the values of different features. Quantum Decision Trees, however, leverage the principles of quantum mechanics â€“ superposition, entanglement, and quantum interference â€“ to enhance this process.

Instead of representing data as bits (0 or 1), QDTs use *qubits*, which can exist in a superposition of both states simultaneously. Entanglement allows for correlations between qubits that cannot be replicated classically, further enhancing the model's expressive power.

The key distinction lies in how the "best" feature to split the data is determined. In classical decision trees, this is typically done using metrics like information gain or Gini impurity.
