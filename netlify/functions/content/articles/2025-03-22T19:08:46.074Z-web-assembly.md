## What is WebAssembly?

WebAssembly, or Wasm, is a binary instruction format designed for execution in modern web browsers.  It's not a programming language itself, but rather a compilation target for languages like C, C++, Rust, Go, and others.  This means you can write high-performance code in your preferred language and compile it down to Wasm, which then runs near-natively in the browser, offering significantly improved speed and efficiency compared to traditional JavaScript. ![A diagram showing the compilation process from various languages to WebAssembly](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1460925895917-afdab827c52f?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **Did you know?**  WebAssembly's initial design goals included security, portability, and efficient execution, leading to its adoption as a crucial component of the modern web ecosystem.

Think of it like this: JavaScript is excellent for its flexibility and ease of use, but it's interpreted, leading to performance limitations for computationally intensive tasks. Wasm, on the other hand, is compiled, allowing for much faster execution.  This makes it ideal for applications that demand high performance, such as games, video editing, and 3D modeling.
