## 3. Overcoming the Von Neumann Bottleneck: A New Architectural Paradigm

Electronic neural networks are often constrained by the Von Neumann bottleneck, a fundamental limitation in the architecture of traditional computers where data transfer between the CPU and memory is a major performance bottleneck. Optical neural networks offer a potential solution by integrating computation and memory within the same optical circuit.

This in-memory computing paradigm eliminates the need for constant data transfer between separate units, significantly speeding up the overall processing time.  ![Diagram illustrating the Von Neumann bottleneck vs. in-memory computing in optical neural networks](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1607723619307-260d7a1e1f12?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
