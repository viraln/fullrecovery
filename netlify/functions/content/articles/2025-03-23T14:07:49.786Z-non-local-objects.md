## Understanding Non-Locality: Beyond Classical Physics

Our everyday experience adheres to the principles of locality: an object can only be directly influenced by its immediate surroundings.  However, the quantum world throws this principle into disarray. Non-locality, a cornerstone of quantum mechanics, describes the phenomenon where two or more entangled particles exhibit correlated behavior, instantaneously influencing each other even across vast distances.
