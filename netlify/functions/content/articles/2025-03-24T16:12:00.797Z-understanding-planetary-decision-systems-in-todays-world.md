## Planetary Protection Protocols and Planetary Defense Systems

Planetary protection protocols are an integral component of any responsible planetary decision system.  These protocols aim to prevent the forward contamination of other celestial bodies with terrestrial life and the back contamination of Earth with extraterrestrial life.  This involves stringent sterilization procedures for spacecraft and equipment, careful selection of landing sites, and rigorous monitoring for any signs of life.  ![An illustration of a spacecraft undergoing sterilization procedures](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1429743305873-d4065c15f93e?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

Planetary defense systems, on the other hand, focus on mitigating the threat posed by near-Earth objects (NEOs).  These systems involve the detection, tracking, and potential deflection of asteroids or comets that pose a collision risk to Earth.  Decision-making within this domain requires sophisticated modeling, rapid response capabilities, and international collaboration.
