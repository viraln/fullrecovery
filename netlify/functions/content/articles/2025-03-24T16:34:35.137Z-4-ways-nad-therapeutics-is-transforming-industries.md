## What is NAD+ Therapy and How Does it Work?

Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide (NAD+) is a coenzyme vital for cellular energy production and numerous metabolic processes.  As we age, our NAD+ levels naturally decline, contributing to cellular dysfunction and age-related decline.  NAD+ therapy aims to replenish these crucial levels, boosting cellular function and potentially mitigating the effects of aging. ![Microscopic image of NAD+ molecule](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1451187580459-43490279c0fa?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

Several methods exist to increase NAD+ levels, including:

* **NAD+ Intravenous (IV) infusions:**  These deliver a high concentration of NAD+ precursors directly into the bloodstream, offering rapid and significant increases.
* **NAD+ injections:** Similar to IV infusions, but administered in smaller doses directly into muscle tissue.
* **NAD+ supplements:** Oral supplements containing NAD+ precursors, like nicotinamide riboside (NR) and nicotinamide mononucleotide (NMN), offer a convenient but potentially less potent approach.

> **EXPERT TIP:**  The optimal method for NAD+ supplementation depends on individual needs and health goals. Consulting with a healthcare professional is crucial before starting any NAD+ therapy.
