## Practical Applications: From Mycelium Bricks to Living Walls

The concept of building-organism hybrids manifests in various forms, each with unique applications:

*   **Mycelium-Based Materials:** Using the root structure of fungi (mycelium) to create strong, lightweight, and biodegradable building blocks. These bricks can be grown into desired shapes and offer excellent insulation.

*   **Living Walls and Green Roofs:** Incorporating vegetation into building facades and rooftops to improve air quality, reduce stormwater runoff, and provide insulation.

*   **Algae Bioreactors:** Integrating algae into building facades to capture carbon dioxide, produce biofuels, and generate electricity. The "BIQ House" in Hamburg, Germany is a pioneering example.

*   **Bio-Concrete:** Incorporating bacteria into concrete to create self-healing properties, extending the lifespan of structures and reducing maintenance costs.

*   **Building Integrated Agriculture (BIA):** Designing buildings with integrated greenhouses, vertical farms, and other agricultural systems to produce food locally.

*   **Self-Healing Buildings:** Research is ongoing into incorporating biological systems that allow buildings to automatically repair cracks and other damage, reducing maintenance and extending lifespan.
