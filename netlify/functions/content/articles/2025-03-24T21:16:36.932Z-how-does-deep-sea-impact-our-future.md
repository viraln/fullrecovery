## The Impact of Climate Change on the Deep Sea

The deep sea isn't immune to the impacts of climate change.  Rising ocean temperatures, ocean acidification, and changes in ocean currents are affecting deep sea habitats and the creatures that inhabit them.  These changes can lead to shifts in species distribution, changes in ecosystem functioning, and even mass extinctions.  The deep sea acts as a significant carbon sink, absorbing large amounts of CO2 from the atmosphere.  However, the increased absorption of CO2 is contributing to ocean acidification, which threatens the survival of many deep sea organisms, especially those with calcium carbonate shells.
