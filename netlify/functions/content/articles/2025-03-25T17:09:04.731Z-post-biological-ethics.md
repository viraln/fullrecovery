##  Ethical Implications of Genetic Engineering

Genetic engineering holds immense promise for treating diseases and improving human health. However, its potential for misuse raises significant ethical concerns:

*   **Germline Editing:**  Altering the human germline (sperm and eggs) permanently changes the genetic makeup of future generations, raising concerns about unforeseen consequences and the potential for eugenics.
*   **Genetic Discrimination:**  The ability to predict an individual's genetic predispositions could lead to discrimination in employment, insurance, and other areas.
*   **Access and Equity:**  The high cost of genetic engineering technologies could exacerbate existing health disparities, creating a two-tiered system of healthcare.

> **EXPERT TIP:**  Robust regulatory frameworks are crucial to ensure the responsible development and use of genetic engineering technologies.  Transparency and public engagement are essential to foster trust and address ethical concerns.
