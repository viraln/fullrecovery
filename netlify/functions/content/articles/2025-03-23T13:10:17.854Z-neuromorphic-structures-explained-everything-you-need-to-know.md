## What is Neuromorphic Computing?

Neuromorphic computing mimics the structure and function of the human brain.  Instead of relying on the traditional von Neumann architecture (which separates memory and processing), neuromorphic systems integrate computation and memory at the hardware level. This allows for massively parallel processing, energy efficiency, and exceptional performance in tasks like pattern recognition and machine learning.  Think of it as building a computer that thinks more like a brain than a traditional digital computer. ![A diagram comparing Von Neumann and Neuromorphic architectures](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1545552987-720aa18145ca?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **DID YOU KNOW?** The term "neuromorphic" was coined by Carver Mead in the 1980s, a pioneer in the field who envisioned building circuits that behave like neurons and synapses.
