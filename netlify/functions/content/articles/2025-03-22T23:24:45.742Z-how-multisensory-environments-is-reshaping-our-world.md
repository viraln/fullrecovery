## The Science Behind Sensory Integration and MSEs

The foundation of MSEs lies in the principles of sensory integration. This neurological process involves the brain's ability to organize and interpret sensory information from the body and the environment. When sensory integration is impaired, it can lead to difficulties with attention, behavior, and motor skills. MSEs provide a controlled environment to help individuals regulate their sensory input, improve sensory processing, and ultimately enhance their overall functioning.  Research consistently demonstrates the positive impact of sensory integration therapy on individuals with autism spectrum disorder (ASD), sensory processing disorder (SPD), and other neurological conditions.
