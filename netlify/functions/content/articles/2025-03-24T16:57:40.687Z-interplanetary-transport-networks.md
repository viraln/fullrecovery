## How ITNs Work: The Mechanics of Low-Energy Trajectories

The core principle behind ITNs lies in finding low-energy trajectories that take advantage of gravitational assists.  Instead of using massive amounts of propellant to reach a destination directly, a spacecraft carefully navigates a series of gravitational "slingshots" around planets and moons.  These maneuvers subtly alter the spacecraft's trajectory, gradually building up velocity and directing it towards its final destination.  This process, sometimes taking years, significantly reduces the overall fuel requirements compared to traditional high-energy transfers. ![An animation showing a spacecraft utilizing gravitational assists from multiple planets.](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1484417894907-623942c8ee29?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
