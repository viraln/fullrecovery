## Non-Standard Model Examples: A Diverse Landscape

The field of non-standard modeling is incredibly rich and diverse. Let's explore some key examples:

* **Agent-Based Modeling (ABM):**  ABM simulates the interactions of autonomous agents to understand emergent behavior. This is powerful for modeling complex social systems, ecological dynamics, and even economic markets. ![A simple diagram illustrating an agent-based model](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1575653932700-fcc34df03761?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
* **Bayesian Networks:** These probabilistic graphical models represent relationships between variables and allow for uncertainty quantification. They are widely used in medical diagnosis, risk assessment, and machine learning.
* **Fuzzy Logic Systems:**  These models deal with uncertainty and imprecision by using fuzzy sets and fuzzy rules.  They are invaluable in control systems, decision support systems, and expert systems.
* **Neural Networks:**  Inspired by the human brain, neural networks are powerful tools for pattern recognition, prediction, and classification. Deep learning, a subfield of neural networks, has revolutionized many domains.
* **Cellular Automata:** These models consist of a grid of cells, each following simple rules.  Despite their simplicity, they can generate complex patterns and are used to model various phenomena, from urban growth to crystal formation.
