## Body Autonomy vs. Bodily Integrity: Understanding the Nuances

While often used interchangeably, body autonomy and bodily integrity are distinct but related concepts.  Bodily integrity refers to the physical inviolability of the body, the right to be free from physical harm and unwanted touching.  Body autonomy, on the other hand, encompasses the broader right to make decisions about one's body, even if those decisions might involve some degree of risk.
