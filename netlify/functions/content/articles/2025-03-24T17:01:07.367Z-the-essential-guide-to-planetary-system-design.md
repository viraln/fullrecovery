##  Creating Realistic Planetary Systems:  Beyond the Basics

Designing a truly realistic system goes beyond simply placing planets in orbits. Consider the following:

* **Orbital Resonances:**  Planets can exert gravitational influence on each other, leading to resonant orbits (e.g., a 2:1 resonance where one planet completes two orbits for every one orbit of the other).
* **Planetary Migration:**  Planets can migrate inward or outward over time due to gravitational interactions with the protoplanetary disk or other planets.
* **Tidal Forces:**  The gravitational pull of a planet on its moon (or a star on its planet) can cause tidal heating, influencing the planet's internal structure and geological activity.
* **Atmospheric Escape:**  Lighter atmospheric gases can escape a planet's gravity over time, depending on factors like temperature and the planet's mass.
