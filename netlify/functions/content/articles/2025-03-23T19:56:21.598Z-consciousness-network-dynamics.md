## Key Theories in Consciousness Network Dynamics

Several prominent theories attempt to explain consciousness network dynamics.  Two leading contenders are:

* **Global Workspace Theory (GWT):** GWT posits that consciousness arises from a "global workspace" – a central area where information from various brain regions is broadcast and integrated.  This allows for flexible and coherent behavior. ![Illustration of Global Workspace Theory](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1542382257-80dedb725088?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

* **Integrated Information Theory (IIT):**  IIT proposes that consciousness is a fundamental property of systems with high levels of integrated information.  The more integrated a system, the higher its level of consciousness. This theory moves beyond purely neural explanations, suggesting that consciousness could potentially arise in other complex systems.
