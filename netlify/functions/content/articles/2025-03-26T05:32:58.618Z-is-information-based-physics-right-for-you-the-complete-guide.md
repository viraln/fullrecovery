## What is Information-Based Physics?  A Deep Dive

Information-based physics, also known as information physics or informational physics, proposes that information is fundamental to the universe.  It suggests that the physical laws we observe are not merely descriptions of reality but rather emergent properties of underlying informational structures. Unlike traditional physics, which focuses on matter and energy, information physics posits that information itself is a primary building block of existence, with matter and energy being secondary manifestations.  ![A stylized representation of bits of information forming complex structures](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1704022472896-c7f2423b3ce7?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress).

This perspective challenges conventional views by suggesting that the universe's apparent complexity arises from the processing and manipulation of information, similar to a complex computer program. This isn't about the information we *observe* about the universe, but about information being the fundamental *stuff* of the universe.

## Information Physics Theory: Key Concepts and Principles

Several key concepts underpin information physics. One is the idea of **algorithmic information theory**, which quantifies the complexity of a system based on the shortest computer program needed to describe it.  Another is the concept of **entropy**, typically associated with disorder in thermodynamics, but in information physics, it represents the amount of information lost or unavailable.
