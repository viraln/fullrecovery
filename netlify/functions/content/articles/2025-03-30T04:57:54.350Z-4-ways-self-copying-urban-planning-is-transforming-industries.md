## What is Self-Copying Urban Planning?

Self-copying urban planning, at its core, is about creating standardized urban development blueprints and modular urban planning components that can be replicated and adapted across different locations. It's not about creating identical cities, but rather about leveraging proven designs, infrastructure layouts, and zoning strategies to accelerate the planning process, reduce costs, and ensure a baseline level of functionality and livability. Think of it as using pre-fabricated building blocks to construct a diverse range of structures, rather than starting from scratch each time.

This approach contrasts sharply with traditional urban planning, which often involves bespoke designs tailored to the specific needs and context of each location. While customization remains crucial, self-copying offers a framework for efficiency and consistency, allowing planners to focus on unique local requirements rather than reinventing the wheel.
