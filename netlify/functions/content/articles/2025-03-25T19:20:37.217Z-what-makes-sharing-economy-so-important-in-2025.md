## Gig Economy vs. Sharing Economy: A Crucial Distinction

While often used interchangeably, the gig economy and the sharing economy are distinct concepts.  The gig economy focuses primarily on short-term, task-based work, often facilitated by digital platforms.  The sharing economy, on the other hand, encompasses a broader range of activities involving the sharing of underutilized assets or skills.  > **EXPERT TIP:**  Think of the gig economy as *a subset* of the sharing economy. A freelance writer on a gig platform is part of the gig economy, which is part of the broader sharing economy.

| Feature          | Gig Economy                               | Sharing Economy                             |
|-----------------|-------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| **Focus**        | Short-term, task-based work              | Sharing underutilized assets or skills       |
| **Examples**     | Freelance writing, delivery services       | Ride-sharing, Airbnb, tool lending           |
| **Ownership**    | Typically, no asset ownership involved     | Often involves sharing owned assets          |
| **Relationship** | Primarily transactional                    | Can foster community and trust              |
