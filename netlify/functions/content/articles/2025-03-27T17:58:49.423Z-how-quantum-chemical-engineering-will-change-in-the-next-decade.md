## Core Principles: Quantum Mechanics for Chemical Engineers

At its heart, quantum chemical engineering applies the principles of quantum mechanics to solve problems in chemical engineering. Unlike classical mechanics, which treats matter as particles, quantum mechanics describes matter as both particles and waves, governed by the Schr√∂dinger equation. This equation describes the behavior of electrons in atoms and molecules, which dictates their chemical properties and interactions. While solving the Schr√∂dinger equation exactly is impossible for all but the simplest systems, various approximation methods, such as Density Functional Theory (DFT) and Hartree-Fock, provide accurate and computationally tractable solutions.

üîë **Key Concepts:**

*   **Schr√∂dinger Equation:** The fundamental equation of quantum mechanics.
*   **Density Functional Theory (DFT):** A widely used method for approximating the electronic structure of materials.
*   **Hartree-Fock:** Another approximation method for solving the Schr√∂dinger equation.
*   **Molecular Dynamics (MD):** Simulating the movement of atoms and molecules over time.
*   **Quantum Mechanics/Molecular Mechanics (QM/MM):** Combining quantum mechanical and classical mechanical methods to study large systems.

Understanding these concepts is crucial for interpreting the results of quantum simulations and applying them to real-world engineering problems.
