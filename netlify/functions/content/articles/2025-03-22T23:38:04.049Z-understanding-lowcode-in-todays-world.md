## What is LowCode and How Does it Work?

LowCode development platforms abstract away much of the complex coding required for traditional software development.  They utilize visual interfaces, drag-and-drop functionalities, and pre-built components to allow developers (both professional and citizen developers) to build applications with minimal hand-coding. > **EXPERT TIP:**  LowCode isn't about eliminating coding entirely. It's about minimizing it, focusing on the core logic and business processes, and leveraging pre-built components for common functionalities.

This approach significantly reduces development time and costs, allowing businesses to respond quickly to changing market demands and internal needs.  The core functionality is achieved through visual modeling, enabling faster prototyping and iteration.

## LowCode vs. NoCode: Understanding the Difference

While often used interchangeably, LowCode and NoCode platforms cater to different needs and skillsets.  LowCode platforms offer a balance between visual development and custom coding, empowering experienced developers to fine-tune applications for optimal performance. NoCode platforms, on the other hand, are designed for users with minimal or no coding experience, focusing solely on visual development and pre-built templates.

| Feature          | LowCode                               | NoCode                                   |
|-----------------|----------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| Coding Required  | Minimal; primarily for customization    | None                                      |
| Target User      | Professional and citizen developers     | Citizen developers, business users        |
| Complexity       | Can handle complex applications          | Primarily for simpler applications        |
| Customization    | High                                   | Limited                                   |
