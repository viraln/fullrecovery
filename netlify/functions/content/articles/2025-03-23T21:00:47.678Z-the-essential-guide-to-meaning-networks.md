## What are Meaning Networks?

Meaning networks, also known as semantic networks, are graphical representations of concepts and the relationships between them.  Imagine a web, where each node represents a concept (like "dog," "mammal," or "loyalty"), and the connections (edges) illustrate the semantic relationships between them (e.g., "dog IS-A mammal," "dog exhibits loyalty"). These networks are not static; they evolve and adapt based on new information and context.  They are a powerful tool for understanding how meaning is constructed and how knowledge is organized. ![A simple visual representation of a meaning network with nodes and edges](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1620969427101-7a2bb6d83273?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **DID YOU KNOW?**  The concept of semantic networks has roots in cognitive psychology, dating back to the 1960s, with researchers trying to model how the human brain organizes knowledge.
