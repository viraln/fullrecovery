The core advantage of molecular digital storage lies in its incredible density. Unlike traditional magnetic or flash storage, which rely on physical limitations, molecular storage utilizes the inherent complexity of molecules, particularly DNA, to encode information.

DNA, for example, can store information at a density orders of magnitude greater than current technologies. A single gram of DNA can theoretically store up to 215 petabytes of data.
