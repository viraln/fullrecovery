## 1. Autopoiesis Definition: The Self-Producing System

Before diving into the revolutionary aspects, let's establish a firm understanding of what autopoiesis *is*.  Coined by Chilean biologists Humberto Maturana and Francisco Varela in the 1970s, autopoiesis (from the Greek *autos* meaning "self" and *poiesis* meaning "creation") describes a system capable of self-production and maintenance. This means the system's components work together to continuously regenerate themselves and the system as a whole.  It's not merely about maintaining a stable state; it's about actively creating and recreating the conditions for its own existence.

> **EXPERT TIP:**  The key to autopoiesis isn't the specific components, but the relationships and interactions between them.  A change in one component can affect the entire system, leading to adaptation and evolution.
