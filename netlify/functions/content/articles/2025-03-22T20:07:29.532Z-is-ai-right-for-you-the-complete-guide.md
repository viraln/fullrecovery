## AI vs. Machine Learning: Understanding the Nuances

While often used interchangeably, AI and machine learning (ML) are distinct concepts. AI is the broader concept encompassing the creation of intelligent machines, while ML is a *subset* of AI focusing on enabling systems to learn from data without explicit programming.  Deep learning, a further subset of ML, uses artificial neural networks with multiple layers to analyze data.

| Feature         | AI                                   | Machine Learning                         | Deep Learning                             |
|-----------------|---------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| **Definition**  | Broad field of creating intelligent systems | Subset of AI focusing on learning from data | Subset of ML using artificial neural networks |
| **Goal**         | Mimic human intelligence              | Learn from data without explicit programming | Learn complex patterns from large datasets |
| **Examples**     | Self-driving cars, chatbots             | Spam filters, recommendation systems      | Image recognition, natural language processing |
