## What is Superposition Ethics? A Quantum Leap in Moral Philosophy

Superposition, in quantum mechanics, describes a state where a particle exists in multiple states simultaneously until measured. Think of Schrödinger's cat, both alive and dead until the box is opened. Superposition ethics borrows this concept, suggesting that in complex moral situations, multiple ethical viewpoints can co-exist. Instead of forcing a binary choice between right and wrong, it encourages considering the validity of different, even conflicting, perspectives.

This approach moves beyond traditional ethical frameworks, like consequentialism (focusing on outcomes) or deontology (focusing on rules), by acknowledging the inherent ambiguity and complexity of many real-world ethical challenges. It's not about finding *the* right answer, but about understanding the spectrum of potential answers and their implications.
