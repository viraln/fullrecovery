## Understanding Mixed Reality Layers: The Foundation of Immersive Experiences

Mixed reality layers, also known as mixed reality overlays, are digital elements—images, text, 3D models, interactive elements—projected onto the real world through devices like AR glasses, smartphones, or even specialized projectors.  These layers aren't simply superimposed; they are intelligently integrated, responding to the user's environment and actions.  Unlike augmented reality (AR), which primarily adds digital content to a real-world view, MR layers often involve more complex interactions and a deeper integration with the physical surroundings.  Think of it as a dynamic, interactive window onto digital information overlaid onto your reality. ![A graphic showing a user interacting with a mixed reality layer showing instructions overlaid on a piece of machinery](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1592478457432-38ed2af3ee03?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
