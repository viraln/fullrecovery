## Circular Economy Business Models: Innovative Approaches

Numerous innovative business models are driving the circular economy transition.  Here are a few key examples:

* **Product-as-a-Service (PaaS):** Companies offer the function of a product rather than the product itself (e.g., subscribing to a printer service instead of buying a printer).
* **Sharing Platforms:** Platforms facilitate the sharing of goods and services, reducing the need for individual ownership (e.g., bike-sharing programs).
* **Industrial Symbiosis:** Businesses collaborate to exchange waste materials as inputs for other processes, creating closed-loop systems. ![A diagram showing different circular economy business models](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1518302057166-c990a3585cc3?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
* **Remanufacturing:** Products are disassembled, repaired, and reassembled to extend their lifespan.
* **Recycling and Upcycling:** Waste materials are processed and transformed into new products.

> **EXPERT TIP:**  Identifying the most suitable business model depends heavily on the specific industry, product, and market context.  Careful analysis is crucial.
