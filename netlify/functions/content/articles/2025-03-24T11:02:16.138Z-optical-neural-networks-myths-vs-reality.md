## How Do Optical Neural Networks Work?

Unlike electronic neural networks that rely on electrical signals to transmit information, optical neural networks leverage light.  This fundamental shift unlocks incredible potential.  Light travels at a significantly faster speed than electrons, leading to dramatically faster processing speeds.  Moreover, light signals can cross each other without interference, enabling parallel processing on an unprecedented scale. ![Diagram illustrating the flow of light signals in an optical neural network](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1606814540563-5c02d62fd409?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

The core components typically include optical waveguides to guide light, optical modulators to control the intensity and phase of light signals, and photodetectors to convert light back into electrical signals for processing and output.  These components are arranged to mimic the interconnected nodes and layers of a traditional artificial neural network.  Different architectures, such as those based on Mach-Zehnder interferometers or diffractive optical elements, are employed to perform various neural network operations.
