## 1. What is a Stellar Engine and How Does it Work?

A stellar engine, in its simplest form, is a hypothetical megastructure designed to harness the energy output of a star for propulsion. Unlike conventional rockets that rely on chemical propellants, stellar engines utilize the star's energy to generate thrust, enabling interstellar travel on scales currently unimaginable.  The concept relies on the principles of momentum transfer; by subtly altering the star's radiation or mass ejection, a stellar engine can generate a minuscule but persistent thrust, slowly accelerating a star (and any attached spacecraft) over vast timescales.

> **DID YOU KNOW?**  The concept of a stellar engine was first seriously proposed by physicist Leonid Shkadov in 1987, introducing the Shkadov thruster as a potential design.

There are several proposed mechanisms for achieving this:  One approach involves using a massive array of solar sails or mirrors to reflect starlight, creating a net directional force. Another involves manipulating the star's stellar wind, directing its outflow to generate thrust.
