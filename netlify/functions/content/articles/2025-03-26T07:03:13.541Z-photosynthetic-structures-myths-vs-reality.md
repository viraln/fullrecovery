## Light-Dependent and Light-Independent Reactions: A Two-Part Process

Photosynthesis is a two-stage process.  The light-dependent reactions occur in the thylakoid membranes, converting light energy into ATP and NADPH.  The light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle) then use this energy in the stroma to convert CO2 into sugars.

![A flowchart illustrating the light-dependent and light-independent reactions.](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1545153987-c456e97455c9?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

**Light-Dependent Reactions:**  This stage involves photosystems I and II, which capture light energy and transfer it to electrons.  This electron flow generates ATP and NADPH.  Water is split (photolysis), releasing oxygen as a byproduct.

**Light-Independent Reactions (Calvin Cycle):**  This stage uses the ATP and NADPH from the light-dependent reactions to fix CO2, converting it into glucose.  This process requires a series of enzyme-catalyzed reactions.
