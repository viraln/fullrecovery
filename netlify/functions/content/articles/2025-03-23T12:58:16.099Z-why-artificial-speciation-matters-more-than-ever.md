## How Does Artificial Speciation Work?

Artificial speciation, also known as artificial selection speciation, accelerates the natural process through human intervention.  Scientists employ various techniques, including selective breeding, genetic engineering (like CRISPR-Cas9), and even artificial environmental manipulation, to drive the divergence of populations and ultimately create new species.  This is fundamentally different from traditional selective breeding, which aims to improve existing traits within a species.  Artificial speciation aims to create entirely new ones.
