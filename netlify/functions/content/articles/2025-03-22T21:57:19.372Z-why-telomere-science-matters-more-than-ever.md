## Understanding Telomeres: The Science Explained

Telomeres are repetitive DNA sequences at the ends of chromosomes, acting like protective caps on shoelaces, preventing fraying and damage.  ![Microscopic image of telomeres](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1486825586573-7131f7991bdd?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)  With each cell division, telomeres naturally shorten. ## Telomere Shortening: The Cellular Clock of Aging

The process of telomere shortening, also known as telomere attrition, is a fundamental aspect of cellular aging.  As telomeres get shorter, cells lose their ability to replicate properly, leading to senescence (cellular aging) and eventually apoptosis (programmed cell death).  This cellular dysfunction contributes to the development of various age-related diseases, including cardiovascular disease, cancer, neurodegenerative disorders, and even certain types of diabetes.  ![Illustration showing telomere shortening over time](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1535127022272-dbe7ee35cf33?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
