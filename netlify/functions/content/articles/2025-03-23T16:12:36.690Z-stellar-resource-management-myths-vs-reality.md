## Myth 1:  Resource Management is Just About Scheduling

**Reality:** Resource management is far more than simply plugging people into a calendar. It's a holistic approach encompassing strategic planning, efficient allocation, performance monitoring, and continuous improvement.  It's about understanding the *value* each resource brings and deploying them where they'll have the biggest impact. ## Myth 2: More Resources Always Equal More Productivity

**Reality:** Throwing more resources at a problem doesn't automatically solve it. This often leads to the "too many cooks in the kitchen" scenario, resulting in communication breakdowns, conflicting priorities, and ultimately, decreased efficiency.  Strategic resource allocation, matched to project needs, is key.  Sometimes, less is more.

> **Did you know?** Studies show that optimal team sizes for project success vary significantly depending on the project's complexity and type.  Overstaffing can reduce individual productivity and increase coordination costs.
