## The Hunt for Habitable Exoplanets: Techniques and Discoveries

The discovery of thousands of exoplanets â€“ planets orbiting stars other than our Sun â€“ has revolutionized our understanding of planetary systems.  Several methods are used to detect these distant worlds, each with its strengths and limitations.

* **Transit Method:** Detecting the slight dimming of a star's light as a planet passes in front of it.  This method is highly effective for finding planets that transit their star from our perspective.
* **Radial Velocity Method:** Observing the wobble in a star's movement caused by the gravitational pull of orbiting planets.  This method is particularly useful for detecting larger planets closer to their stars.
* **Direct Imaging:** Directly observing a planet's light, separate from its host star. This is extremely challenging, requiring advanced technology and is currently limited to large, young planets.

In 2025, the Kepler and TESS missions have yielded a treasure trove of data, leading to the confirmation of numerous exoplanets, some within the habitable zones of their stars.
