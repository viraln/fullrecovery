## What is Neuromorphic Computing?

Neuromorphic computing is a revolutionary approach to computing that mimics the structure and function of the human brain.  Unlike traditional computers that rely on sequential processing, neuromorphic systems employ massively parallel processing using interconnected networks of artificial neurons and synapses.  This allows them to handle complex, unstructured data efficiently, excelling in tasks where traditional computers fall short.  The core idea is to build hardware that operates in a similar way to the brain, rather than simply simulating brain function in software.

> **DID YOU KNOW?** The term "neuromorphic" was coined by Carver Mead in the 1980s, a pioneer in the field who envisioned building electronic circuits that directly mirrored the biological processes of the brain.
