## 2.  Gravity Tractor: A Gentle Nudge for Asteroid Deflection

Unlike the forceful impact of kinetic impactors, the gravity tractor offers a gentler, more controlled approach.  This method involves stationing a spacecraft near a potentially hazardous asteroid, using its gravitational pull to gradually alter the asteroid's trajectory over an extended period. ![An illustration of a gravity tractor spacecraft orbiting an asteroid.](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1686747506706-67418fbd612c?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

This technique requires significantly more lead time than kinetic impactors, potentially decades, but it offers greater precision and control.  It's particularly suitable for larger asteroids where a single, powerful impact might be less effective or even counterproductive.

*  The advantages of the gravity tractor include:
    *  Minimal disruption to the asteroid's structure.
    *  Potential for course correction over a long timeframe.
    *  Reduced risk of unintended consequences.

*  However, challenges include:
    *  The need for very long lead times.
    *  The high cost and technological complexity of long-duration space missions.
