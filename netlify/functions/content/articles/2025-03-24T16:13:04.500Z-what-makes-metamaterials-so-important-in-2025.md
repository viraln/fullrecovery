## What Are Metamaterials?  Unveiling the Science of Artificial Structures

Metamaterials aren't found in nature; they are artificially engineered materials with properties not found in naturally occurring materials.  These properties stem not from the composition of the material itself, but from its carefully designed structure at a subwavelength scale. Think of it like this: a single brick doesn't have the strength of a well-constructed brick wall. Similarly, the properties of a metamaterial arise from the arrangement and interaction of its microscopic building blocks.  These structures manipulate electromagnetic waves (light, radio waves, etc.) in unconventional ways, leading to unprecedented functionalities. ![Microscopic image of a metamaterial structure](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1451187580459-43490279c0fa?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **DID YOU KNOW?** The concept of metamaterials was first theoretically proposed in the 1960s, but it wasn't until the late 1990s and early 2000s that advancements in nanofabrication techniques made their creation possible.

## Key Properties and Characteristics of Metamaterials: Beyond Natural Limits

Unlike conventional materials, metamaterials can exhibit properties like negative refractive index, cloaking, and perfect lensing.  This is achieved by manipulating the material's permittivity (how easily it allows electric fields to pass through) and permeability (how easily it allows magnetic fields to pass through).
