## What is Star Lifting?

Star Lifting, also known as Silhouette Soft, is a minimally invasive procedure designed to lift and contour the face and neck. Unlike traditional facelifts, it doesn't involve incisions or lengthy recovery periods.  Instead, it utilizes absorbable threads strategically placed under the skin to lift and reposition sagging tissues.  These threads, made of a biocompatible material called polylactic acid (PLA), are inserted through tiny incisions, creating a lifting effect that gradually improves over time. ![Close-up image of Star Lifting threads](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1565120319398-da008925fc1d?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **Did you know?**  The PLA threads used in Star Lifting are the same biocompatible material used in many dissolvable stitches, ensuring biodegradability and safety.

## The Star Lifting Procedure: A Step-by-Step Guide

The Star Lifting procedure is relatively quick and straightforward. Here's a breakdown of what to expect:

1. **Consultation:**  A thorough consultation with a qualified practitioner is crucial to determine candidacy and tailor the treatment plan to your specific needs. ![Doctor consulting with a patient](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1476111021705-ac3b3304fe20?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
2. **Local Anesthesia:**  A local anesthetic is typically administered to numb the treatment area, ensuring comfort throughout the procedure.
3. **Thread Insertion:**  Tiny incisions are made, and the specially designed threads are inserted using a cannula (a thin, blunt-ended needle). The practitioner strategically places the threads to lift and support sagging tissues.
4. **Thread Adjustment:**  Once the threads are in place, the practitioner carefully adjusts them to achieve the desired lifting and contouring effect.
5. **Post-Procedure Care:**  After the procedure, the practitioner will provide detailed instructions for aftercare, including minimizing strenuous activity and following a specific skincare routine.
