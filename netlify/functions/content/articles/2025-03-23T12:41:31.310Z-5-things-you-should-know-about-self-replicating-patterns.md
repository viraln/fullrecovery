## 3.  How Do Self-Replicating Patterns Work? Deconstructing the Process

The core components of a self-replicating system typically include:

1. **Information Storage:**  This is the blueprint containing the instructions for creating a copy. DNA in biology, code in computer science, and even the shape of a crystal structure serve this purpose.

2. **Replication Mechanism:** This is the process by which the information is copied and translated into a new instance.  This involves enzymes in biology, compilers in computer science, and physical processes in crystal growth.

3. **Resource Acquisition:** The system needs resources to build a new copy.  This can involve energy, raw materials, or computational power.

4. **Construction Process:** This is the actual assembly of the new copy based on the information and acquired resources.
