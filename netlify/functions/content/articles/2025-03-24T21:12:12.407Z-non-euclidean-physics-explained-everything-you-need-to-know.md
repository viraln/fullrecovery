## Understanding Non-Euclidean Geometry: The Foundation

Before we delve into the physics, we need a solid grasp of the underlying mathematical framework: non-Euclidean geometry.  Unlike Euclidean geometry, which assumes a flat, two-dimensional plane, non-Euclidean geometries describe spaces with different properties.  Two primary types dominate:

* **Hyperbolic Geometry:** Imagine a saddle. This geometry describes surfaces where parallel lines diverge. The angles of a triangle in hyperbolic space add up to *less* than 180 degrees. ![Illustration of a hyperbolic surface](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1707510917424-2d66055df14d?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

* **Elliptic Geometry:**  Think of the surface of a sphere. Parallel lines eventually converge (like lines of longitude).  In elliptic geometry, the angles of a triangle add up to *more* than 180 degrees. ![Illustration of a triangle on a sphere](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1636913747874-b3f65cee1bdb?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
