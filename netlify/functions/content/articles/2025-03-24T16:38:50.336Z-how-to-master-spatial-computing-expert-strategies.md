## What is Spatial Computing? Understanding the Fundamentals

Spatial computing refers to the interaction between humans and computers in a three-dimensional environment. Unlike traditional computing, which relies primarily on two-dimensional screens, spatial computing leverages the physical space around us, enabling more natural and intuitive interactions.  This involves technologies like augmented reality (AR), virtual reality (VR), mixed reality (MR), and computer vision, all working in concert to create immersive and responsive digital experiences. ![A graphic illustrating the difference between traditional computing and spatial computing](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1451187580459-43490279c0fa?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **EXPERT TIP:**  Understanding the nuances between AR, VR, and MR is crucial. AR overlays digital information onto the real world, VR creates entirely simulated environments, and MR blends the real and virtual worlds seamlessly.
