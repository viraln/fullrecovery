## Understanding Exoskeleton Technology: Beyond Science Fiction

Exoskeletons, also known as wearable robots, are external frameworks that enhance human capabilities.  These aren't the metallic suits of science fiction; modern exoskeletons are sophisticated pieces of engineering, combining robotics, materials science, and human physiology to create powerful and adaptable tools.  They work by leveraging a complex system of sensors, actuators, and control systems to assist or augment human movement, strength, and endurance.  ![A diverse range of exoskeletons, showcasing different designs and applications](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1738669465523-1aa4d153c126?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

## Types of Exoskeletons: A Diverse Landscape

The world of exoskeletons is incredibly diverse, with different types designed for specific applications.  Broadly, they can be categorized as:

* **Passive Exoskeletons:** These don't require an external power source; instead, they use springs and levers to assist with movement. They are typically lighter and simpler than active exoskeletons but offer less support.
* **Active Exoskeletons:** These use motors and batteries to provide powered assistance, enabling greater strength and endurance augmentation. They are typically more expensive and complex but offer superior performance.
* **Medical Exoskeletons:** Designed for rehabilitation and assisting individuals with mobility impairments, these can help with walking, standing, and other movements.  ![A medical exoskeleton assisting a patient with rehabilitation](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1720459370581-65c9abe0e641?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
* **Industrial Exoskeletons:**  These are used in workplaces to reduce strain and improve productivity in physically demanding jobs such as construction, manufacturing, and logistics.  They often focus on upper body support or lower body assistance.
* **Military Exoskeletons:** Designed to enhance the strength, endurance, and protection of soldiers, these are often heavier and more robust than other types.
