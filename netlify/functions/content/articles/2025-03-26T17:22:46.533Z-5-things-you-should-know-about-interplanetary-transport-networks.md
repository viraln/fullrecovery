Traditional interplanetary missions rely on large amounts of propellant to accelerate and decelerate spacecraft, and change their orbital parameters. ITNs, on the other hand, exploit the gravitational fields of celestial bodies to achieve these changes with minimal propulsive effort.

Low energy transfers often involve spending a significant amount of time in transit, sometimes years, as the spacecraft slowly spirals its way towards its destination. The concept of a *free return trajectory* is closely related to low energy transfers. A free return trajectory is a path that, if followed correctly, will naturally bring a spacecraft back to its starting point without requiring any further propulsive maneuvers. These trajectories are often used in lunar missions to provide a built-in safety mechanism in case of engine failure.
