## The Architecture of a Cosmic Neural Network

Building a GSNN requires a sophisticated architecture capable of handling the sheer volume of data and computational complexity involved.  Key architectural considerations include:

* **Distributed Computing:** GSNNs rely heavily on distributed computing frameworks, such as those offered by cloud providers like Google Cloud Platform, AWS, and Azure.  This allows for parallel processing across thousands of machines, enabling the training and inference of massive models.
* **Data Handling:** Efficient data pipelines are crucial.  These pipelines must ingest, preprocess, and distribute petabytes of astronomical data from various sources (e.g., telescopes, simulations).  Techniques like data compression and distributed storage are essential.

## Common Challenges and Solutions

Despite their potential, GSNNs present significant challenges:

* **Computational Cost:** Training and running GSNNs requires immense computational resources, making them expensive and inaccessible to many researchers.  **Solution:**  Leveraging cloud computing resources and optimizing algorithms.
* **Data Bias:**  Bias in astronomical datasets can lead to biased predictions.  **Solution:**  Careful data curation and preprocessing, alongside techniques to mitigate bias during training.
* **Model Interpretability:**  Understanding the internal workings of a complex GSNN can be difficult.  **Solution:**  Developing explainable AI (XAI) techniques to enhance model transparency.
* **Data Scarcity in Certain Domains:** Some astronomical phenomena are rare, leading to limited training data. **Solution:**  Synthetic data generation and transfer learning techniques.
