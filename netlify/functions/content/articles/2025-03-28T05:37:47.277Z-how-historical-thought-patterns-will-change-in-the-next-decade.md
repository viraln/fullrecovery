## The Shifting Sands of Historical Interpretation

The traditional, often Eurocentric, narrative of history is crumbling. We are witnessing a powerful push for more inclusive and diverse perspectives. This isn't just about adding marginalized voices; it's about fundamentally rethinking the *framework* through which we understand historical events. The next decade will see a continued and intensified focus on:

*   **Decolonizing History:** Examining historical events through the lens of formerly colonized nations and peoples, challenging existing power dynamics and narratives.
*   **Intersectionality:** Recognizing the complex interplay of race, gender, class, and other social categories in shaping historical experiences.
*   **Global History:** Moving beyond national boundaries to understand interconnectedness and global flows of people, ideas, and resources.
