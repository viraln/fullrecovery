## Multi-Agent System Architecture: Building Blocks of Success

The architecture of a MAS is crucial for its effectiveness. Several common architectures exist, each with its own strengths and weaknesses:

* **Centralized Architecture:** A central control unit coordinates the actions of all agents.  Simple to implement but lacks robustness and scalability.
* **Decentralized Architecture:** Agents operate independently, coordinating through peer-to-peer communication. More robust and scalable but complex to manage.
* **Hybrid Architecture:** Combines aspects of centralized and decentralized architectures, offering a balance between control and autonomy.  ![A comparison chart illustrating the three architectures](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1597268489488-28be7e3e2baf?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress).
