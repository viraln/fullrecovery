## Defining Space Law: More Than Just Treaties

Space law, also known as **international space law**, is the body of legal principles, treaties, and customary rules that govern activities in outer space. It's not a monolithic entity; instead, it's a dynamic field constantly evolving to address new technologies and activities.  This includes everything from the launch and operation of satellites to the exploration and exploitation of celestial bodies. ## Liability in Space Law: Who Pays When Things Go Wrong?

Determining liability in space-related incidents is complex. The Liability Convention provides a framework, but ambiguities remain, particularly in cases involving multiple actors (e.g., a satellite collision involving satellites from different countries or a private company's launch causing damage).
