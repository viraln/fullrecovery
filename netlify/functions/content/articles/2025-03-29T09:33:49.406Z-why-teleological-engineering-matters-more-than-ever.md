## What is Teleological Engineering?

Teleological engineering, at its core, is **goal-oriented engineering**. It's the practice of designing systems and products with a pre-defined purpose and outcome in mind. The term "teleological" comes from the Greek word "telos," meaning "end" or "purpose." Unlike traditional engineering, which often focuses on efficiency and functionality, teleological engineering prioritizes the *reason* behind the design. It asks: "What problem are we *really* trying to solve?" and "What is the *intended* function?"

This approach requires a deep understanding of the problem space, the stakeholders involved, and the potential consequences of the design. It also demands a holistic perspective, considering not only the immediate function but also the long-term impact on society and the environment. Teleological Systems Engineering takes this principle and applies it to vast and interconnected systems, ensuring each component contributes to the overall, intended purpose.
