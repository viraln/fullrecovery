## How Does Non-Invasive BCI Work?

Non-invasive BCIs measure brain activity from outside the skull, avoiding the need for surgery.  This typically involves sensors placed on the scalp, such as electroencephalography (EEG) caps, which detect electrical signals produced by neurons.  These signals are then amplified, processed, and translated into commands for external devices.  ![Diagram showing EEG cap and signal processing](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1635713044873-8341db112d07?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress).

> **EXPERT TIP:** The accuracy and resolution of non-invasive BCIs are constantly improving, leading to more precise and reliable control.

Different techniques exist, including magnetoencephalography (MEG), which measures magnetic fields produced by brain activity, and near-infrared spectroscopy (NIRS), which measures changes in blood flow. Each technique offers unique advantages and limitations in terms of spatial resolution, temporal resolution, and cost.
