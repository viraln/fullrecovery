## The Quantum Measurement Problem:  A Deeper Dive

The core of the quantum measurement problem lies in understanding *how* this collapse occurs.  The standard Copenhagen interpretation suggests that the wave function (the mathematical description of a quantum system) collapses upon measurement, but it doesn't explain the mechanism behind this collapse.  This has led to numerous interpretations, including:

* **Many-Worlds Interpretation:**  This suggests that every measurement causes the universe to split into multiple branches, each representing a possible outcome.
* **Consistent Histories:** This approach focuses on the entire history of a system, avoiding the need for a specific moment of wave function collapse.
* **Objective Collapse Theories:** These theories propose modifications to quantum mechanics to account for spontaneous wave function collapse.
