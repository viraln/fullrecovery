## What Are Reality Filters?

Reality filters, also known as augmented reality (AR) filters, are digital overlays superimposed onto a live video or image feed, modifying the user's perception of reality.  They leverage computer vision and machine learning to track and interact with the real world in real-time.  This allows for a wide array of effects, from simple cosmetic changes to complex interactive experiences.  Unlike virtual reality (VR), which creates entirely artificial environments, AR filters enhance the existing reality. ![Example of a simple AR filter overlaying a cat's face with cartoonish glasses](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1622042002773-a6f2913a6e84?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **DID YOU KNOW?** The very first rudimentary forms of reality filters can be traced back to early image processing techniques in the 1960s, though the technology has exploded in capabilities since the rise of smartphones.
