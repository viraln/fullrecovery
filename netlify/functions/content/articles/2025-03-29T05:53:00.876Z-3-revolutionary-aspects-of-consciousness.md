## 2. Unveiling the Neural Correlates: Mapping the Brain's Consciousness Network

One of the most significant advances in consciousness research is the identification of neural correlates of consciousness (NCCs). These are the specific brain activity patterns that are consistently associated with conscious experience. By mapping these patterns, we're gaining a deeper understanding of how consciousness arises from the physical brain.

![Consciousness visualization](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1635497611324-129442752063?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

*   **Global Workspace Theory (GWT):** This theory proposes that consciousness arises when information is broadcast globally across the brain, making it available to various cognitive processes. Research supports the idea that specific brain regions, such as the prefrontal cortex and parietal cortex, play a crucial role in this global broadcasting.
*   **Integrated Information Theory (IIT):** IIT suggests that consciousness is directly related to the amount of integrated information a system possesses. The more complex and interconnected a system is, the more conscious it is likely to be. This theory has implications for understanding consciousness in artificial intelligence.
*   **The Default Mode Network (DMN):** As mentioned earlier, the DMN is a network of brain regions that is active when we're not focused on external tasks. It's associated with self-referential thought, mind-wandering, and introspection. Disrupting the DMN, as seen with psychedelics, can lead to profound alterations in consciousness.
