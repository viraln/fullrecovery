## Understanding Time-Differentiated Species and Speciation

Time-differentiated species, or *temporal species*, are defined by their distinct evolutionary lineages separated by significant periods. This separation isn't just geographical (allopatric speciation) or ecological (sympatric speciation); it's fundamentally temporal, often revealed through molecular clock dating techniques.  The process of *time-differentiated speciation* involves the gradual accumulation of genetic differences over extended periods, leading to reproductive isolation and the emergence of distinct species. ![A phylogenetic tree illustrating time-differentiated speciation](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1451187580459-43490279c0fa?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
