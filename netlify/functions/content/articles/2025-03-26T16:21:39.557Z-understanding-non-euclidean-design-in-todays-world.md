## What is Non-Euclidean Geometry and Design?

Euclidean geometry, the geometry we learn in school, operates under the assumption that parallel lines never meet. Non-Euclidean geometry, however, challenges this fundamental postulate, exploring geometries where parallel lines can intersect (hyperbolic geometry) or where there are no parallel lines at all (spherical geometry).  This seemingly abstract concept translates into breathtaking design possibilities. Imagine buildings that curve and twist in impossible ways, logos that defy perspective, and user interfaces that offer a completely new spatial experience.  Non-Euclidean design leverages these alternative geometric systems to create visually stunning and conceptually innovative work. ![A comparison image showing Euclidean vs. Non-Euclidean shapes](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1476357471311-43c0db9fb2b4?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
