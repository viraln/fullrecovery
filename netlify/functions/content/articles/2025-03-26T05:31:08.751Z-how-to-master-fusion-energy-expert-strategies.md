## How Does Fusion Energy Work?

Fusion energy mimics the process powering the sun and stars: combining light atomic nuclei (like isotopes of hydrogen) to form a heavier nucleus (like helium), releasing immense energy in the process.  Unlike fission, which splits atoms, fusion merges them. This process requires overcoming the electrostatic repulsion between positively charged nuclei, demanding extreme temperatures and pressures.  These conditions are typically achieved using magnetic confinement or inertial confinement techniques.
