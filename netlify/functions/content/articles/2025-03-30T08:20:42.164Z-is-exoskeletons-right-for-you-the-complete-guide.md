## What Exactly Are Exoskeletons? Demystifying Wearable Robotics

At their core, exoskeletons are wearable robotic devices designed to augment human capabilities. They act as external frameworks, providing support, strength amplification, and assistance with movement. Often referred to as "powered exoskeletons," these devices use a combination of sensors, actuators, and control systems to mimic and enhance the natural biomechanics of the human body.

Exoskeletons come in a variety of forms, ranging from full-body suits to targeted devices that focus on specific limbs or joints. They can be powered, relying on electric motors or hydraulics for assistance, or passive, utilizing springs and dampeners to redistribute weight and reduce strain. The choice between powered and passive depends largely on the intended application and the level of assistance required. ![A side-by-side comparison of a full-body powered exoskeleton and a passive back support exoskeleton](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1738669465523-1aa4d153c126?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **EXPERT TIP:** Understanding the difference between powered and passive exoskeletons is crucial. Powered exoskeletons offer significant strength augmentation, while passive exoskeletons focus on support and injury prevention.
