Electric scooters and bike-sharing programs have stepped in to bridge this gap, offering a readily available, affordable, and eco-friendly solution.

## 3. Reshaping Urban Design: A City Built for People, Not Cars

Micromobility is not just about individual transportation; it's about fundamentally reshaping urban design to prioritize people over cars. This includes creating dedicated micromobility lanes, expanding pedestrian zones, and rethinking parking infrastructure. As cities embrace micromobility, they are becoming more walkable, bikeable, and accessible for all residents.

![Micromobility visualization](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1596111481242-202979790f93?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

**Step 1:** Cities are investing in dedicated micromobility lanes to ensure the safety and efficiency of scooter and bike riders. These lanes separate micromobility vehicles from cars and pedestrians, reducing the risk of accidents.

**Step 2:** Urban planners are expanding pedestrian zones and creating more public spaces that are accessible by foot, bike, and scooter. This promotes a more vibrant and engaging urban environment.

**Step 3:** Cities are rethinking parking infrastructure to accommodate micromobility vehicles. This includes creating designated scooter and bike parking areas, as well as repurposing existing parking spaces for micromobility use.

> **EXPERT TIP:** Successful integration of micromobility requires a holistic approach that considers infrastructure, regulations, and public education.

This shift towards a more people-centered urban design has numerous benefits:

*   **Increased Economic Activity:** Pedestrian-friendly areas tend to attract more businesses and visitors.
