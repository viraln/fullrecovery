## Brainwashing Techniques: The Coercive Side of Influence

Brainwashing, or coercive persuasion, involves systematic and often brutal methods to dismantle an individual's existing beliefs and replace them with new ones.  Historically, this has been used in cults, political regimes, and during wartime.  Techniques often involve:

1. **Isolation:** Cutting off contact with external influences.
2. **Sleep deprivation:** Weakening resistance and making individuals more susceptible.
3. **Repetitive indoctrination:**  Constant exposure to the desired ideology.
4. **Reward and punishment:**  Reinforcing desired behaviors and punishing dissent.

> **EXPERT TIP:**  Recognizing the signs of coercive control is crucial to protecting oneself and others.  Look for patterns of isolation, manipulation, and control over resources.

Psychological manipulation is far more common than many realize.  It's a subtle form of influence that exploits vulnerabilities and biases to achieve a desired outcome.  Examples include:

* **Triangulation:**  Creating conflict between individuals to gain power.
