## What is Consciousness? A Definition and Exploration

Defining consciousness is notoriously difficult.  It's not a simple thing to pin down.  While we all experience it, explaining it precisely remains a challenge.  A common definition describes consciousness as the state of being aware of and responsive to one's surroundings.  This encompasses subjective experience (qualia), self-awareness, and the ability to interact with the world in a meaningful way. ![A brain scan highlighting active regions](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1510034141778-a4d065653d92?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **EXPERT TIP:**  It's crucial to distinguish between different levels of consciousness.  Wakefulness is simply being awake, while awareness involves processing information and having subjective experiences.  Self-awareness is a higher level, involving understanding that *you* are experiencing these things.

Different schools of thought offer diverse perspectives.  Materialism posits that consciousness arises solely from physical processes in the brain.  Dualism proposes a separation between mind and body, suggesting consciousness exists independently.  Integrated information theory (IIT) suggests consciousness arises from the complexity and integration of information within a system.  The debate continues!
