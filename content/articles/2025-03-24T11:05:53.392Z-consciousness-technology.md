## How Does Consciousness Technology Work?

The core principle lies in detecting and interpreting brain activity.  This is achieved through various methods:

1. **Electroencephalography (EEG):** Measures electrical activity in the brain using electrodes placed on the scalp.  This is a relatively inexpensive and non-invasive method, commonly used in neurofeedback.
2. **Magnetoencephalography (MEG):** Measures magnetic fields produced by electrical activity in the brain.  MEG offers better spatial resolution than EEG.
3. **Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI):**  Measures brain activity by detecting changes in blood flow.  fMRI provides excellent spatial resolution but is expensive and less portable than EEG or MEG.
4. **Near-Infrared Spectroscopy (NIRS):**  Measures brain activity by detecting changes in blood oxygenation.  NIRS is a relatively portable and less expensive method than fMRI.

These technologies are used to gather data, which is then processed and analyzed to understand brain states and potentially influence them through feedback mechanisms or external stimulation.
