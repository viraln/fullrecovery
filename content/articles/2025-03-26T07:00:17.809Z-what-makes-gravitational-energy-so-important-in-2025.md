## What is Gravitational Energy Explained?

Gravitational energy, simply put, is the potential energy an object possesses due to its position in a gravitational field.  The more massive an object and the higher it is positioned relative to a gravitational source (like the Earth), the greater its gravitational potential energy.  This energy is converted into kinetic energy as the object falls, accelerating towards the source.  ![Illustration of an object falling and converting gravitational potential energy into kinetic energy](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1451847251646-8a6c0dd1510c?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **EXPERT TIP:** Understanding the distinction between gravitational potential energy (GPE) and gravitational energy is crucial. GPE is the energy associated with the object's position, while gravitational energy encompasses the total energy within a system influenced by gravity.
