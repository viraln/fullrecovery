## What Are Decision Markets and How Do They Work?

Decision markets, also known as prediction markets in some contexts, are systems where participants buy and sell contracts whose value depends on the outcome of a future event or decision. Unlike traditional prediction markets that primarily focus on forecasting, decision markets are explicitly designed to influence the decision-making process itself.  The price of these contracts reflects the collective belief of participants regarding the likelihood of different outcomes.  This aggregated wisdom can provide valuable insights for decision-makers, offering a data-driven approach to complex problems. ![A graphic illustrating the basic mechanics of a decision market, showing buyers and sellers trading contracts](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1642543348781-ed9c6d67ed20?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **EXPERT TIP:**  The key difference between decision markets and prediction markets lies in their intent. Prediction markets aim to forecast; decision markets aim to inform and improve decision-making.

## Prediction Markets vs. Decision Markets: A Clear Distinction

While often used interchangeably, prediction and decision markets have crucial distinctions:

| Feature          | Prediction Market                               | Decision Market                                     |
|-----------------|-------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|
| **Primary Goal** | Forecasting future events                       | Informing and improving decision-making processes |
| **Outcome Impact**| No direct influence on the outcome              | Can directly influence the chosen outcome           |
| **Participants**  | Often speculative investors                     | Stakeholders, experts, and decision-makers         |
| **Contract Value**| Based solely on the accuracy of the prediction | Based on the outcome and its impact on the decision |
