## What are Quantum Materials?

Quantum materials are substances that exhibit unique and often unexpected properties stemming directly from quantum mechanical effects. Unlike classical materials, where properties are largely determined by the average behavior of electrons, quantum materials display behavior governed by the intricate quantum interactions of individual electrons, leading to phenomena like superconductivity, topological insulation, and colossal magnetoresistance.  These properties arise from the interplay of electrons' spins, charges, and orbital motions at the atomic level, influenced by factors like crystal structure, composition, and external stimuli (temperature, pressure, magnetic fields). ![Microscopic image of a quantum material crystal lattice](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1742576033320-d6e7c5352192?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **Did you know?** Some quantum materials exhibit properties that defy classical physics, like the ability to conduct electricity with zero resistance (superconductivity) or to conduct electricity on their surface but not in their interior (topological insulators).

## Quantum Material Applications: A Glimpse into the Future

The applications of quantum materials are vast and rapidly expanding.  Current and potential applications include:

* **Quantum Computing:**  Quantum materials are essential for building qubits, the fundamental building blocks of quantum computers.  Materials like superconductors are crucial for maintaining qubit coherence.
