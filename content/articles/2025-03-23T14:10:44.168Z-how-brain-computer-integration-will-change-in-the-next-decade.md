##  How Does Brain-Computer Interface Work?

BCIs work by detecting and interpreting brain activity.  This involves placing sensors on the scalp (non-invasive) or directly implanting electrodes into the brain (invasive).  These sensors measure electrical signals produced by neurons, which are then processed by a computer algorithm.  This algorithm translates these signals into commands that can control external devices, such as robotic arms, computers, or even prosthetic limbs. ![A simplified diagram showing how a BCI translates brain signals into commands](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1484417894907-623942c8ee29?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

The complexity of the algorithm and the resolution of the sensors greatly affect the precision and speed of the BCI.  More advanced systems use machine learning algorithms to improve their ability to interpret brain signals over time, adapting to the individual user's unique brain patterns.
