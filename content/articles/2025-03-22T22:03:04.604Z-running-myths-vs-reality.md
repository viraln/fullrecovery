## Myth #2: Running Will Inevitably Lead to Injuries

**Reality:**  While running carries a risk of injury, it's significantly lower than many assume.  Proper training, appropriate footwear, and attention to body mechanics significantly reduce this risk. Many injuries are caused by overtraining, improper form, or inadequate warm-ups and cool-downs.

## Myth #3: You Need a Strict Running Plan from Day One

**Reality:**  For beginners, a structured running plan can be beneficial, but starting with a focus on consistency and building a base level of fitness is crucial.  Many find success with a "Couch to 5k" program, gradually increasing their running time and distance.

**How to start a running plan:**

1.  **Start with walk/run intervals:** Alternate walking and running for shorter periods.
2.  **Gradually increase your running time:**  As you get fitter, gradually increase the duration of your runs and reduce the walking intervals.
3.  **Listen to your body:** Don't push yourself too hard, especially in the beginning.
4.  **Find a running buddy:** Accountability and motivation can make a big difference.
