## 1.  Solar Radiation Management: Cooling the Planet's Fever

Solar radiation management (SRM) techniques aim to reduce the amount of sunlight reaching the Earth's surface, thus counteracting global warming.  The most discussed SRM method is stratospheric aerosol injection (SAI), mimicking the cooling effect of large volcanic eruptions.  This involves injecting reflective particles, such as sulfate aerosols, into the stratosphere.

> **EXPERT TIP:** While promising, SAI's potential side effects, such as altered rainfall patterns and ozone depletion, require extensive research and careful consideration before deployment.

## 2. Carbon Dioxide Removal:  Cleaning Up the Atmosphere

Carbon dioxide removal (CDR) technologies focus on actively extracting CO2 from the atmosphere.  These methods range from natural solutions like afforestation and reforestation to technological approaches like direct air capture (DAC).

![A picture showcasing a direct air capture plant.](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1598538085731-05ab942009af?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

DAC plants use chemical processes to capture CO2 directly from ambient air.  The captured CO2 can then be stored underground (carbon capture and storage, or CCS) or utilized in various industrial processes (carbon capture and utilization, or CCU).  While DAC is currently expensive, technological advancements and economies of scale are expected to reduce costs significantly in the coming years.  The potential for scaling up CDR technologies is massive, offering a pathway to actively reduce atmospheric CO2 concentrations.
