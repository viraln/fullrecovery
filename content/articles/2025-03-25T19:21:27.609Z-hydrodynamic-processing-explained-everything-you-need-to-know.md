## Key Mechanisms: Cavitation and Shear

Two primary mechanisms drive the effectiveness of hydrodynamic processing:

* **Hydrodynamic Cavitation:** This involves the formation, growth, and implosion of vapor-filled bubbles within a liquid under high pressure and velocity conditions. The implosion of these bubbles generates intense localized pressure and temperature changes, leading to powerful effects like emulsification, particle size reduction, and cell disruption.

* **Hydrodynamic Shear:** This refers to the frictional forces between adjacent layers of a fluid in motion.  High shear rates can induce significant mechanical stress on materials within the fluid, leading to phenomena such as homogenization, mixing, and the breakdown of larger molecules or structures.

##  Types of Hydrodynamic Processing Equipment

The specific equipment used in hydrodynamic processing varies depending on the application and desired outcome.  Common types include:

* **High-pressure homogenizers:** These devices utilize high-pressure pumps to force liquids through narrow orifices, creating intense shear and cavitation. They are widely used in the food and pharmaceutical industries for homogenization and particle size reduction.
* **Ultrasonic processors:** These employ ultrasonic waves to generate cavitation within a liquid, providing a highly effective method for disrupting cells and extracting valuable compounds.
* **Rotor-stator mixers:** These devices use a rotating rotor within a stationary stator to create high shear forces, ideal for mixing viscous liquids and breaking down clumps.
* **Microfluidizers:** These advanced systems utilize microchannels to generate extremely high shear rates and cavitation, resulting in exceptional control over particle size and distribution.
