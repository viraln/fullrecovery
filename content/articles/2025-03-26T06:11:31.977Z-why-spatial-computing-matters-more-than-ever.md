## What is Spatial Computing?

Spatial computing refers to technologies that enable computers to understand, interact with, and manipulate the physical environment. Unlike traditional computing which primarily deals with flat screens and keyboards, spatial computing involves three-dimensional interactions using technologies like augmented reality (AR), virtual reality (VR), mixed reality (MR), and computer vision.  It's about creating immersive and interactive experiences that bridge the gap between the digital and physical worlds.
