## Quantum Hypercomputing vs. Classical Computing

The difference between quantum hypercomputing and classical computing is not simply one of degree, but of kind. Classical computers rely on bits, representing information as either 0 or 1. Quantum computers use qubits, leveraging superposition to represent 0, 1, or a combination of both simultaneously. Quantum hypercomputers go further, potentially harnessing entanglement to link qubits across vast distances, creating a massively parallel processing system with unprecedented computational power.
