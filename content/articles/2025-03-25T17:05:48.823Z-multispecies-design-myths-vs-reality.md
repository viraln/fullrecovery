## What is Multispecies Design?  Debunking the Common Myths

Multispecies design isn't just about adding a few birdhouses to a building or planting some trees in a park. It's a fundamental shift in design philosophy, acknowledging that humans are not the sole inhabitants of the planet and that our built environments profoundly impact all species.  It's about creating spaces and systems that foster biodiversity, enhance ecological resilience, and promote mutually beneficial relationships between humans and other species.

**Myth 1:** Multispecies design is only for "tree huggers."  **Reality:** It's a practical approach to design that benefits everyone.  Increased biodiversity leads to improved air and water quality, enhanced resilience to climate change, and even increased property values.

**Myth 2:**  It's too expensive and complicated. **Reality:**  While initial investments might be higher in some cases, long-term benefits often outweigh the costs.  Innovative materials and design strategies are constantly emerging to make multispecies design more accessible.

**Myth 3:** It's a purely aesthetic consideration. **Reality:** Aesthetics are a component, but the core is functional ecology and the well-being of all species.
