## Transhumanism Ethics: Navigating the Moral Maze

The ethical implications of transhumanism are profound and complex.  Key concerns include:

* **Inequality:**  Access to life-extending and enhancing technologies will likely be unevenly distributed, exacerbating existing social and economic inequalities.  This could create a two-tiered society where the wealthy enjoy enhanced lives while the poor are left behind.

## Transhumanism vs Posthumanism: A Key Distinction

While often used interchangeably, transhumanism and posthumanism represent distinct concepts.  Transhumanism focuses on enhancing existing human capabilities through technology, aiming for a gradual transition. Posthumanism, on the other hand, envisions a future where humans have fundamentally transcended their biological limitations, potentially becoming something entirely different.  This could involve merging with machines, creating artificial consciousness, or evolving into entirely new forms of life.

| Feature          | Transhumanism                               | Posthumanism                                    |
|-----------------|-----------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| **Goal**         | Enhance human capabilities                    | Transcendence of human limitations               |
| **Approach**     | Gradual technological enhancement            | Radical transformation                           |
| **Humanity**    | Retains core human characteristics             | Potentially loses core human characteristics      |
| **Timeline**     | Near- to mid-term future                      | Long-term or indefinite future                   |
