## What is Star Lifting?

Star lifting, also known as thread lifting or suspension, is a minimally invasive cosmetic procedure that uses absorbable threads to lift and tighten sagging skin.  These specialized threads, often made of PDO (polydioxanone) or other biocompatible materials, are inserted under the skin using fine needles. Once in place, they create a lifting effect, improving facial contours and reducing wrinkles. Unlike traditional facelifts, star lifting requires minimal recovery time and produces natural-looking results. ![Close-up image of star lifting threads being inserted](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1565120319398-da008925fc1d?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **DID YOU KNOW?** The threads used in star lifting not only lift the skin but also stimulate collagen production, leading to further improvements in skin texture and tone over time.

## Star Lifting Procedure: A Step-by-Step Guide

The star lifting procedure is typically performed in a clinic or doctor's office under local anesthesia.  Here's a general overview:

1. **Consultation:** A thorough consultation with a qualified practitioner is crucial to determine suitability and discuss desired outcomes.
2. **Anesthesia:** Local anesthesia is applied to numb the treatment area.
3. **Thread Insertion:**  Fine needles are used to insert the threads under the skin, strategically placed to lift and tighten specific areas. ![Diagram showing thread placement during star lifting](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1476111021705-ac3b3304fe20?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
4. **Thread Adjustment:** The practitioner carefully adjusts the threads to achieve the desired lifting effect.
5. **Post-Procedure Care:** Instructions for aftercare, including avoiding strenuous activity and following specific skincare routines, are provided.

The entire procedure usually takes between 30 minutes to an hour, depending on the treatment area and the number of threads used.
