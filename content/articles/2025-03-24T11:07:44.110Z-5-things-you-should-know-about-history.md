## 2.  History is Subjective: Multiple Perspectives Matter

A common misconception is that history is an objective account of the past.  However, historical accounts are always shaped by the perspectives and biases of the historians who write them.  Different sources offer varying interpretations of the same events, highlighting the importance of considering multiple perspectives.

For example, the American Revolution can be viewed from the perspective of the colonists fighting for independence, the British seeking to maintain control, or the Native American tribes whose lands were impacted by the conflict.  Each perspective offers a unique and valuable understanding of the events.

> **DID YOU KNOW?** The way history is written has changed dramatically over time.  Early historical accounts often focused on the actions of kings and queens, neglecting the experiences of ordinary people.  Modern historians strive for a more inclusive approach.
