## Diving Deep: Hyperbolic, Elliptic, and Beyond

Non-Euclidean geometry isn't a single entity; it's a diverse landscape of possibilities. The two most prominent examples are:

*   **Hyperbolic Geometry:** Imagine a saddle-shaped surface. In hyperbolic space, parallel lines diverge exponentially, and the angles of a triangle sum to *less* than 180 degrees. It’s a space of negative curvature. A famous model is the Poincaré disk model.

*   **Elliptic Geometry (Riemannian Geometry):** Think of the surface of a sphere. In elliptic space, there are no parallel lines – all lines eventually intersect. The angles of a triangle sum to *more* than 180 degrees. This space has positive curvature.

But the story doesn't end there. More exotic Non-Euclidean spaces exist, defined by different curvature tensors and topological properties. These include spaces with torsion, spaces with more complex curvature profiles, and even spaces that are locally Euclidean but globally Non-Euclidean.
