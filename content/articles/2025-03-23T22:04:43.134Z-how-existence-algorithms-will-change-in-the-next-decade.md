## What are Existence Algorithms?

Existence algorithms are a relatively new class of algorithms designed to determine the existence of a solution to a problem *before* attempting to find that solution.  Unlike traditional search algorithms (like A*, Dijkstra's, or even brute-force methods) that focus on finding optimal or satisfactory solutions, existence algorithms prioritize proving or disproving the possibility of a solution's existence. This seemingly small distinction has enormous implications for computational efficiency and problem-solving strategy.  Think of it like this:  before embarking on a long and potentially fruitless search for a lost item, an existence algorithm would first try to determine if the item is even *in* the house.
