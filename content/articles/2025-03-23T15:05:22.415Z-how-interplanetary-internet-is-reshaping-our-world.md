## Deep Space Network: The Backbone of Interplanetary Communication

The Deep Space Network (DSN), a global network of radio antennas operated by NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, currently forms the foundation of interplanetary communication.  These massive antennas, located in California, Spain, and Australia, transmit and receive signals to and from spacecraft across the solar system.  However, the DSN has its limitations.  Its bandwidth is limited, and the signal delays can be substantial, particularly for distant spacecraft.
