## Understanding the Driving Forces: Plate Tectonics and Beyond

Geological restructuring is primarily driven by plate tectonics, the theory explaining the movement of Earth's lithosphere â€“ the rigid outer shell encompassing the crust and upper mantle.  These colossal plates, constantly shifting at rates of a few centimeters per year, interact at their boundaries, resulting in a variety of geological phenomena.  ![Illustration of tectonic plates and their boundaries](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1451187580459-43490279c0fa?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

*   **Divergent Boundaries:** Plates move apart, creating mid-ocean ridges and new crust.
*   **Convergent Boundaries:** Plates collide, leading to mountain building (orogeny), subduction (one plate sliding under another), and volcanic activity.
*   **Transform Boundaries:** Plates slide past each other horizontally, causing earthquakes.

However, geological restructuring isn't solely defined by plate tectonics.  Other significant processes include:

*   **Isostatic Adjustment:**  The vertical movement of Earth's crust in response to changes in mass, like glacial melting or sediment deposition.
*   **Earth's Crust Deformation:**  Changes in the shape and structure of the Earth's crust due to tectonic stress, erosion, and other factors. This can manifest as folding, faulting, and fracturing.
*   **Erosion and Weathering:**  These processes continuously sculpt the Earth's surface, shaping landscapes and impacting sediment transport.
