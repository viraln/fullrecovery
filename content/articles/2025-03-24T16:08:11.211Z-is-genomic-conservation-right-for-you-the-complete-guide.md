##  Latest Trends and Developments in Genomic Conservation

The field of genomic conservation is rapidly evolving:

* **Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS):**  The cost and speed of genome sequencing are continually decreasing, making it more accessible for conservation projects.
* **Ancient DNA (aDNA):**  Analyzing DNA from extinct or historical specimens provides crucial insights into evolutionary history and helps inform conservation strategies for closely related species.
* **Environmental DNA (eDNA):**  Detecting DNA from environmental samples (water, soil) allows for non-invasive monitoring of species populations and biodiversity. ![A scientist collecting a water sample for eDNA analysis](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1693673763242-15b226156a6c?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
