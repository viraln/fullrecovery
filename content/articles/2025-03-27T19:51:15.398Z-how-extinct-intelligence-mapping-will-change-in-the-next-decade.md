## The Dawn of Paleopsychology: Mapping Extinct Minds

Paleopsychology, or the study of extinct minds, is no longer a fringe science. Fuelled by technological advancements and interdisciplinary collaboration, it's becoming a more rigorous and data-driven field. The core objective is to infer cognitive abilities from the physical remains, artifacts, and environmental contexts left behind by extinct species. The foundation of paleopsychology rests on several pillars:

*   **Archaeological Evidence:** Analyzing tools, art, burial sites, and settlement patterns to infer behavior and cognitive complexity.
*   **Comparative Neuroscience:** Examining brain endocasts (fossilized impressions of the brain) and comparing them to the brains of living species.
*   **Cognitive Modeling:** Creating computational models that simulate the cognitive processes of extinct species based on available data.
*   **Evolutionary Psychology:** Using principles of evolution to understand how cognitive abilities evolved over time.
*   **Genetics:** Identifying genes associated with cognitive traits and tracing their evolutionary history.

> **EXPERT TIP:** The success of extinct intelligence mapping hinges on the integration of data from multiple disciplines. No single source of evidence is sufficient to reconstruct a complete cognitive profile.
