## The Science Behind Time Distortion:  Neurological and Psychological Mechanisms

Our experience of time isn't a direct reflection of physical time; it's constructed by our brain.  Several key areas are involved:

* **The Hippocampus:**  Crucial for memory consolidation, the hippocampus plays a key role in our perception of time's duration.  The more detailed the memory trace of an event, the longer it seems to have lasted.
* **The Cerebellum:**  Involved in motor control and coordination, the cerebellum also contributes to our internal clock, influencing our perception of temporal order and duration.
* **Dopamine and Norepinephrine:**  These neurotransmitters modulate our attention and arousal levels.  Higher levels are associated with faster time perception, while lower levels can make time seem to slow down.
