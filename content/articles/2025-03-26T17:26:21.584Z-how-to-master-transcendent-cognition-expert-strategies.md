While transcendent cognition may sound esoteric, it has a firm basis in neuroscience. Research shows that altered states of consciousness are associated with specific changes in brain activity, including shifts in brainwave patterns, increased gamma wave activity, and alterations in the default mode network (DMN).

The DMN, responsible for self-referential thought, tends to quiet down during transcendent experiences, allowing for a greater sense of unity and interconnectedness.
