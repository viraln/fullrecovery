## 2. Advancing Climate Engineering Solutions: A Global Imperative

Planetary engineering's impact on climate change mitigation is arguably its most significant contribution to date.  While the concept of geoengineering remains controversial, the development of technologies to counteract climate change is accelerating.  These technologies, born from the need to address planetary-scale environmental challenges, are having a ripple effect on other industries.

One example is the development of advanced carbon capture and storage (CCS) technologies.  Initially conceived for potential use on Mars to create a breathable atmosphere, CCS is now being deployed in various industrial settings to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. ![A carbon capture facility](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1581092580497-e0d23cbdf1dc?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

Another area of significant advancement is solar radiation management (SRM), which involves techniques to reflect sunlight and reduce global temperatures. While still largely theoretical, research into SRM is driving innovation in materials science and atmospheric modeling, with potential applications in weather modification and renewable energy technologies.

> **DID YOU KNOW?** Some proposed SRM methods involve deploying stratospheric aerosols, similar to the effects of large volcanic eruptions.
