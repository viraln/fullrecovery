## Many-Worlds Morality: A New Ethical Framework?

The implications of MWI for ethics are profound and still being explored. Traditional ethical frameworks, largely based on a single, linear reality, struggle to accommodate the idea of branching timelines.  If every action creates multiple versions of yourself, experiencing both positive and negative consequences, how do we determine right from wrong?  Does moral responsibility even exist in a multiverse?

Some argue that MWI necessitates a shift away from consequentialist ethics (judging actions based on outcomes) towards virtue ethics (focusing on character and intent).  Since you can't control the outcomes in all branching universes, focusing on acting virtuously in *your* branch becomes paramount.  Others suggest a form of "ensemble ethics," where moral evaluation considers the aggregate consequences across all branching universes.
