## Key Stages of Self-Identity Development

While the process is fluid, several key stages generally characterize self-identity development:

1. **Childhood (0-12):**  Early identity formation is heavily reliant on caregivers and immediate family.  Children begin to understand themselves through the lens of others' perceptions.
2. **Adolescence (13-19):**  This period is often marked by an "identity crisis," as individuals explore different roles, values, and beliefs, seeking to establish a distinct sense of self separate from their family.
3. **Young Adulthood (20-39):**  Identity continues to evolve as individuals navigate relationships, careers, and life goals.  Commitment to certain roles and values solidifies.
4. **Adulthood (40+):**  While identity remains fluid, this stage often involves consolidating life choices and reflecting on past experiences, leading to a more integrated sense of self.

> **DID YOU KNOW?**  Erik Erikson's theory of psychosocial development, which outlines eight stages of development throughout the lifespan, provides a valuable framework for understanding identity formation across the entire life course.
