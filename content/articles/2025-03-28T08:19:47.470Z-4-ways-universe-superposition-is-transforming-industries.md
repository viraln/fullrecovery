## What is Universe Superposition? Untangling the Multiverse

At its core, universe superposition, also referred to as multiverse superposition, is an extension of the quantum mechanical principle of superposition applied to entire universes. In quantum mechanics, superposition describes the ability of a quantum system (like an electron) to exist in multiple states simultaneously until measured. Before measurement, the electron is in a probabilistic combination of all possible states. The Copenhagen interpretation suggests that the act of measurement collapses the superposition, forcing the electron to "choose" one state.

Universe superposition extends this concept to the entire cosmos. The "many-worlds interpretation" (MWI) of quantum mechanics, championed by Hugh Everett III, suggests that instead of collapsing, each quantum measurement causes the universe to split into multiple universes, each representing a different possible outcome. These universes exist simultaneously, superposed upon each other.
