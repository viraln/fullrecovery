## What is Programmable Matter?

Programmable matter (PM) refers to materials whose properties, such as shape, stiffness, conductivity, and color, can be altered dynamically and autonomously.  Unlike traditional materials, PM isn't static; it can reconfigure itself in response to external stimuli or internal programming. This transformative capability is achieved through the integration of microscopic or macroscopic components, often incorporating computation, sensing, and actuation capabilities.  Think of it as a collection of tiny robots, each with its own intelligence, working together to create a larger, adaptable structure. ![Microscopic view of programmable matter components](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1494192785370-e91e091d544f?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **DID YOU KNOW?** The concept of programmable matter draws inspiration from biological systems, such as cells that self-organize into complex tissues and organs.
