## What is Spatial Computing?

Spatial computing is the convergence of physical and digital worlds, creating interactive experiences anchored in real-world locations and environments. Unlike traditional computing, which primarily focuses on flat screens and keyboards, spatial computing leverages technologies like augmented reality (AR), virtual reality (VR), mixed reality (MR), and computer vision to overlay digital information onto the real world or immerse users in entirely simulated environments. This enables intuitive interactions through gestures, voice commands, and even eye tracking.  ![A person interacting with a holographic display](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1484807352052-23338990c6c6?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

## Spatial Computing Technology: The Building Blocks of Immersion

Several core technologies underpin spatial computing:

* **Augmented Reality (AR):** Overlays digital information onto the real world, enhancing our perception. Think Pokémon Go or Snapchat filters.
* **Virtual Reality (VR):** Creates fully immersive, computer-generated environments, transporting users to different worlds.
* **Mixed Reality (MR):** Blends the real and virtual worlds, allowing digital objects to interact with the physical environment.
* **Computer Vision:** Enables computers to "see" and interpret the world, understanding objects and their spatial relationships.
* **Sensors and Tracking:**  Essential for accurately positioning users and objects in space, providing accurate feedback.
* **Haptic Feedback:** Adds a sense of touch to digital interactions, enhancing realism and immersion.
