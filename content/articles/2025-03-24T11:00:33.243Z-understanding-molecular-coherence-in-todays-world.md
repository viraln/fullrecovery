## Molecular Coherence Explained: Quantum Mechanics at Play

To truly grasp molecular coherence, we need to delve into the quantum realm.  Unlike classical physics, quantum mechanics dictates that molecules can exist in superposition, meaning they can be in multiple states simultaneously. Coherence arises when these multiple states remain correlated, maintaining a specific phase relationship. This phase relationship is crucial; its disruption leads to decoherence, destroying the synchronized behavior.

> **EXPERT TIP:** Think of it like waves. Coherent waves are in phase, leading to constructive interference (amplification). Decoherent waves are out of phase, resulting in destructive interference (weakening).
