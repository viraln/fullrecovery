## 2. Bioethics Issues in Healthcare: Navigating Ethical Dilemmas in Medicine

Healthcare presents a constant stream of ethical dilemmas.  Consider these examples:

* **End-of-life care:**  Decisions about life support, euthanasia, and physician-assisted suicide raise profound ethical questions about the value of life and the role of healthcare professionals.
* **Organ transplantation:**  The scarcity of organs creates ethical challenges in allocation, raising questions of fairness and justice. Who gets priority?  Should wealth or social status play a role?
* **Genetic testing and screening:**  The potential for genetic discrimination and the implications of knowing one's genetic predispositions raise complex ethical issues.
* **Informed consent:** Ensuring patients fully understand the risks and benefits of treatments before they agree to them is crucial, but obtaining truly informed consent can be challenging, especially with complex medical procedures.



**Did you know?** The Nuremberg Code, developed after World War II, established fundamental ethical principles for human experimentation, including the necessity of informed consent.

## 3. Bioethics and Artificial Intelligence: The Emerging Frontier

The rapid advancement of AI in healthcare presents a new wave of ethical challenges.  AI algorithms used in diagnosis, treatment planning, and drug discovery raise concerns about:

* **Bias and fairness:**  Algorithms trained on biased data can perpetuate and amplify existing inequalities in healthcare.
* **Transparency and accountability:**  The "black box" nature of some AI systems makes it difficult to understand how decisions are made, hindering accountability.
* **Data privacy and security:**  The use of sensitive patient data in AI systems raises concerns about privacy violations and data breaches.
* **Autonomy and human control:**  The increasing reliance on AI in healthcare raises questions about the role of human judgment and decision-making.
