##  Types of Planetary Shield Systems: A Comprehensive Overview

Planetary shield systems aren't a single technology; rather, they encompass a range of approaches, each with its strengths and weaknesses.  These can be broadly categorized as:

* **Deflection:** This involves altering the trajectory of an incoming NEO, nudging it away from a collision course with Earth.  Methods include:
    * **Kinetic Impactor:**  Crashing a spacecraft into the asteroid to change its velocity.  (Successfully demonstrated by NASA's DART mission in 2022)
    * **Gravity Tractor:**  Using a spacecraft's gravitational pull over extended periods to gradually alter the asteroid's course.
    * **Nuclear Option:**  Employing a nuclear explosion near or on the asteroid to vaporize material and create thrust. This remains controversial due to potential risks.

* **Disruption:** This involves breaking the asteroid into smaller, less dangerous fragments.  This is primarily considered for larger asteroids where deflection might be impractical.  Again, nuclear methods are often considered, but non-nuclear methods, such as powerful lasers, are also being researched.

* **Mitigation:**  This encompasses strategies to minimize the impact's effects, such as evacuation plans, early warning systems, and development of impact-resistant infrastructure.  This is crucial even with successful deflection, as some fragments might still reach Earth.
