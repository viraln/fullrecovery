Cybernetics, in its simplest form, is the study of control and communication in both animals and machines.  The term, coined in 1948 by Norbert Wiener, focuses on the similarities between these systems, leading to the concept of feedback loops and self-regulation.  A **cybernetic organism**, therefore, is any living being whose biological functions are augmented or enhanced through the integration of technological components. This ranges from simple prosthetic limbs to sophisticated neural interfaces.  The **cybernetics definition** is constantly expanding as technology advances, pushing the boundaries of what's possible.
