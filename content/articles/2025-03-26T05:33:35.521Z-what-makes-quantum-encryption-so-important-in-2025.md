## Quantum Encryption Explained: The Power of Quantum Key Distribution (QKD)

Quantum encryption, specifically Quantum Key Distribution (QKD), leverages the fundamental principles of quantum mechanics to secure communication. Unlike classical encryption, which relies on complex mathematical problems, QKD relies on the laws of physics themselves.  The core of QKD is the transmission of cryptographic keys using individual photons. Any attempt to intercept these photons alters their quantum state, immediately alerting the sender and receiver to eavesdropping. ![Diagram illustrating the principles of Quantum Key Distribution](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1638787377553-0547687f3a87?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

This makes QKD incredibly secure; any interception is immediately detectable, ensuring the integrity of the key exchange.  Once a secure key is established, it can then be used to encrypt and decrypt data using traditional symmetric encryption algorithms.
