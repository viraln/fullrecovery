## Understanding Resonance: How Does Resonance Work?

At its core, resonance is the tendency of a system to oscillate with greater amplitude at some frequencies than at others.  These frequencies are known as resonant frequencies, or natural frequencies.  When an external force applies energy at a system's resonant frequency, the system absorbs that energy, leading to increased amplitude of oscillation. This phenomenon isn't limited to swings. It applies to everything from musical instruments (acoustic resonance) to bridges (mechanical resonance) and even atoms.
