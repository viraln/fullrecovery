## What is Graphene and How Does it Differ from Graphite?

Graphene, at its core, is a single layer of carbon atoms arranged in a hexagonal lattice.  Think of it as a single sheet extracted from graphite, the material found in your pencil lead.  ![Microscopic image of graphene lattice](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1625993051424-114a461d406a?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress) Graphite, however, consists of many layers of graphene stacked on top of each other, held together by weak van der Waals forces. This fundamental difference accounts for the vastly superior properties of graphene.

> **Did you know?**  A single sheet of graphene is so thin that it's essentially two-dimensional!
