## 2. Biomimicry: Learning from Ocean Solutions

Biomimicry is the practice of emulating nature's designs and processes to solve human problems. The ocean, with its millions of years of evolutionary refinement, offers a treasure trove of inspiration. Ocean-based biomimicry focuses specifically on mimicking the strategies and designs found in marine organisms and ecosystems.

Consider the design of a whale's flippers. Researchers have studied the tubercles (bumps) on humpback whale flippers to improve the efficiency of wind turbines and airplane wings. These tubercles disrupt airflow, reducing drag and increasing lift. This simple yet elegant design has the potential to revolutionize various industries.

Another example is the self-cleaning properties of shark skin. Shark skin is covered in tiny denticles that prevent the growth of bacteria and algae. This has inspired the development of antimicrobial surfaces for hospitals and other environments where hygiene is critical. Companies are now developing coatings based on shark skin that reduce the need for harsh cleaning chemicals.
