## What is a Tesseract and Why is it Difficult to Build?

A tesseract, also known as a hypercube, is the four-dimensional analogue of a cube. Just as a cube is constructed from six square faces, a tesseract is constructed from eight cube cells. Visualizing and understanding a tesseract is challenging because our brains are wired to perceive only three spatial dimensions.
