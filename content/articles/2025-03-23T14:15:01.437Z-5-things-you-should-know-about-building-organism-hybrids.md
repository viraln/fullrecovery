## 4.  Common Challenges and Solutions in Building Biohybrid Systems

Despite the immense potential, building effective building-organism hybrids presents significant challenges:

* **Biocompatibility:** Ensuring that the artificial components are compatible with living organisms is crucial to prevent adverse reactions.  Careful selection of materials and surface modifications are essential.

* **Signal Transduction:**  Efficient communication between the biological and artificial components is vital.  Developing robust interfaces for signal transmission and processing is a key challenge.

* **Scalability and Manufacturing:**  Producing biohybrid systems at scale can be challenging.  Developing efficient and cost-effective manufacturing processes is essential for widespread adoption.

* **Long-Term Stability:**  Maintaining the functionality of biohybrid systems over extended periods can be difficult.  Strategies to improve the longevity and stability of these systems are crucial.
