## What is Intelligence Scaffolding?

Intelligence scaffolding, closely related to the broader concept of learning scaffolding, is a strategic approach that provides temporary support structures to learners, enabling them to tackle complex tasks and concepts that would otherwise be beyond their current capabilities.  Unlike traditional teaching methods that often focus solely on the final outcome, intelligence scaffolding emphasizes the *process* of learning, breaking down challenging tasks into smaller, manageable steps.  It's about building a bridge to understanding, rather than simply throwing learners into the deep end. ![A visual representation of scaffolding supporting a building under construction](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1580063665421-4c9cbe9ec11b?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

## Intelligence Scaffolding Strategies and Techniques

A range of strategies can be employed to create effective intelligence scaffolding:

* **Modeling:** Demonstrating the task or process step-by-step.
* **Think-alouds:** Verbalizing one's thought processes while performing a task.
* **Guided practice:** Providing support and feedback during practice sessions.
* **Chunking:** Breaking down large tasks into smaller, manageable units.
* **Graphic organizers:** Using visual aids to structure information and ideas.
* **Peer collaboration:** Encouraging learners to work together and learn from each other.
* **Questioning techniques:**  Using open-ended questions to stimulate critical thinking.
