## What is Molecular Computing?

Molecular computing harnesses the power of molecules, particularly DNA, RNA, and proteins, to perform computations.  Unlike traditional silicon-based computers that rely on electronic signals, molecular computers utilize the intricate interactions and reactions of these biological molecules. This allows for massive parallelism, meaning many calculations can occur simultaneously, potentially leading to exponentially faster processing speeds.  ![Microscopic image of DNA strands](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1731940579405-56812bdffda3?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
