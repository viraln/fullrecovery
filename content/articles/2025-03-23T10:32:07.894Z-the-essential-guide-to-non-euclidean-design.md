## Understanding the Foundations: Euclidean vs. Non-Euclidean Geometry

Before diving into the exciting world of non-Euclidean design, let's establish a clear understanding of the underlying geometries.  Euclidean geometry, the geometry we learn in school, operates on flat, two-dimensional planes and assumes parallel lines never meet.  Non-Euclidean geometry, however, challenges this fundamental assumption. It explores geometries where parallel lines can meet (spherical geometry) or never meet (hyperbolic geometry).
