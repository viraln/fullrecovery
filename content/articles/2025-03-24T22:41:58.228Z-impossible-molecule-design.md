## Impossible Molecule Structures:  Exploring Novel Architectures

The pursuit of impossible molecules often involves exploring novel molecular architectures, pushing beyond traditional bonding patterns and geometries.

* **Hypervalent Molecules:** These molecules contain atoms with more than eight valence electrons, challenging the octet rule.  Their synthesis and stability are often difficult to achieve.
* **Cage-like Structures:**  These molecules exhibit unique topological features, with atoms arranged in complex three-dimensional frameworks.  Their synthesis often requires intricate multi-step reaction sequences.
* **Polyhedral Boranes and Carboranes:** These clusters of boron and carbon atoms form unique structures with unusual bonding characteristics, offering exciting possibilities for materials with unique properties.
* **Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs):**  These highly porous materials are created by combining metal ions with organic linkers.
