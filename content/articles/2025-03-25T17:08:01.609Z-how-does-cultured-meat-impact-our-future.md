## Understanding Cultured Meat: From Cells to Steak

Cultured meat is produced by cultivating animal cells in a laboratory setting.  Instead of raising and slaughtering animals, scientists take a small sample of animal cells (muscle, fat, etc.) and place them in a nutrient-rich medium. These cells then multiply and grow into muscle tissue, which is then processed into meat products like burgers, sausages, or even whole cuts of steak. ![Microscopic image of cultured meat cells growing in a bioreactor](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1675774644393-7bcfa424dec7?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

This process eliminates the need for traditional animal farming, drastically reducing the environmental impact and ethical concerns associated with the industry.  The core principle is simple: replicate the natural process of muscle growth, but outside of a living animal. This opens up a world of possibilities for customizing the meat's nutritional profile and reducing the risk of foodborne illnesses.
