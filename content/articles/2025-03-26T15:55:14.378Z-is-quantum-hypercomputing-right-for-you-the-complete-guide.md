## Quantum Hypercomputing vs. Classical Computing

The difference between quantum hypercomputing and classical computing is monumental. Classical computers use bits representing 0 or 1. Quantum computers use qubits, leveraging superposition to represent 0, 1, or both simultaneously.  Quantum hypercomputing takes this further, by connecting multiple quantum computers to work together on a single problem. This results in an exponential increase in processing power, enabling the solution of problems currently intractable even for the most powerful supercomputers.
