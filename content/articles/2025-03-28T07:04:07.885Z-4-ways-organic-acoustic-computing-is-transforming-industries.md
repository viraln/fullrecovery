## What is Organic Acoustic Computing?

Organic acoustic computing, also known as bio-acoustic computing, is a novel approach to computation that uses biological materials and sound waves to perform calculations. Unlike traditional electronic computers that rely on transistors and electrical signals, organic acoustic computers leverage the unique properties of biological matter to manipulate and process acoustic signals. This can involve using cells, proteins, or even entire microorganisms to create sound-based neural networks.

The field draws inspiration from how biological systems naturally process information. For example, the human ear converts sound waves into electrical signals that the brain interprets. Organic acoustic computing aims to mimic these processes, using biological components to detect, modulate, and transmit sound waves in a controlled manner.
