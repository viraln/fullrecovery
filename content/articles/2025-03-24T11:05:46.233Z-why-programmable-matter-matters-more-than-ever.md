## What is Programmable Matter?

Programmable matter (PM) refers to materials whose physical properties and functionalities can be altered and controlled dynamically.  Unlike traditional materials with fixed properties, PM can change its shape, stiffness, color, conductivity, and other characteristics in response to external stimuli or programmed instructions. This is achieved through a network of microscopic or nanoscopic components, often including actuators, sensors, and computational elements, which interact and coordinate their actions. Think of it as a swarm of tiny robots working together to create a larger, adaptable structure. ![Microscopic image of programmable matter components](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1494192785370-e91e091d544f?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **DID YOU KNOW?**  The concept of programmable matter draws inspiration from biological systems, such as the self-assembling behavior observed in cells and organisms.

The core concept involves individual units, often called "programmable atoms" or "programmable particles," that can communicate and interact with each other. These units can be anything from tiny robots to specially engineered materials.  The collective behavior of these units allows the material to adopt different configurations and functionalities, leading to a wide range of potential applications.
