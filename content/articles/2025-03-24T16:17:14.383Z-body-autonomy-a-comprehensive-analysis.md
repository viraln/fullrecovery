## Body Autonomy vs. Bodily Integrity: A Crucial Distinction

While often used interchangeably, body autonomy and bodily integrity have subtle yet important differences.  Bodily integrity focuses on the physical inviolability of the body â€“ protection against physical harm or unwanted interference.  Body autonomy is broader, encompassing not only physical integrity but also the right to make decisions about one's body and health, including accepting or refusing medical interventions.  Think of bodily integrity as the *protection* of the body, and body autonomy as the *control* of the body.
