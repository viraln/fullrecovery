## 1. What is ESG?  A Deep Dive into the Criteria

ESG stands for Environmental, Social, and Governance. It represents a framework for evaluating a company's performance across these three dimensions.  It's not about charity; it's about assessing how effectively a company manages its impact on the world and its long-term sustainability.

* **Environmental (E):** This focuses on a company's impact on the environment.  Key considerations include greenhouse gas emissions, waste management, resource consumption, pollution, and biodiversity.  Companies with strong environmental performance demonstrate a commitment to reducing their carbon footprint and mitigating climate risks. ![Graph showing the growth of ESG investing](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1451187580459-43490279c0fa?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

* **Social (S):** This encompasses a company's relationships with its employees, customers, suppliers, and the wider community.  Factors include labor standards, human rights, diversity and inclusion, product safety, and community engagement.  Socially responsible companies prioritize ethical practices and contribute positively to society.

* **Governance (G):** This relates to a company's leadership, executive pay, audits, internal controls, and shareholder rights.  Strong governance structures ensure transparency, accountability, and ethical decision-making.  Companies with robust governance frameworks are better positioned to manage risks and create long-term value.

> **Did you know?**  The term "ESG" gained significant traction in the early 2000s, but its roots can be traced back to the growing awareness of social responsibility in the 1970s and 1980s.
