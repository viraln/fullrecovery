Programmable matter refers to materials whose properties, shape, and functionality can be altered dynamically and autonomously. Unlike traditional materials with fixed characteristics, programmable matter consists of numerous microscopic or macroscopic components, often referred to as "atoms" or "tiles," that can interact and rearrange themselves according to a programmed set of instructions. This programmability is achieved through various mechanisms, including magnetic fields, light, electric fields, or even chemical reactions.
