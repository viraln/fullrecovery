## Understanding the Fundamentals: Policy Definition and Types

What is policy, exactly?  A simple **policy definition** is a course of action adopted or proposed by a government, organization, or individual.  It's a deliberate choice, a roadmap outlining how to achieve specific goals.  We encounter policies daily, often without realizing it.  They range from the grand scale of international treaties to the micro-level rules within our workplaces.

We can broadly categorize policies into several key types:

* **Public Policy:** These are government-created policies affecting society, like environmental regulations or healthcare initiatives.  ![A photo depicting a government official signing a bill into law](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1451187580459-43490279c0fa?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
* **Company Policy:** These are internal rules and guidelines set by businesses to govern employee conduct, operations, and ethical standards.  Examples include data privacy policies or social media usage policies.
* **Institutional Policy:** These policies govern the operations of institutions such as universities, hospitals, or non-profits.
* **Individual Policy:**  This refers to personal guidelines and strategies an individual employs to achieve their objectives.
