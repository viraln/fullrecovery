## 2. Blockchain Technology for Enhanced Data Security and Integrity

Blockchain, initially known for its role in cryptocurrencies, is transforming web security.  Its decentralized, immutable nature makes it an ideal platform for securing sensitive data and verifying its authenticity.

## 3. The Shift Towards Serverless Architecture for Improved Resilience

Traditional web servers are vulnerable to attacks targeting their operating systems and configurations.  Serverless architectures, on the other hand, significantly reduce this attack surface.  Instead of managing servers, developers deploy code as independent functions that are executed on demand by cloud providers.

This eliminates the need for continuous server maintenance and patching, reducing the risk of vulnerabilities.  Additionally, serverless architectures are inherently scalable, making them more resilient to denial-of-service (DoS) attacks.

![A comparison graphic of traditional server architecture vs. serverless architecture](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1573164574230-db1d5e960238?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **EXPERT TIP:**  Carefully vet your cloud provider's security measures before migrating to a serverless architecture. Ensure they have robust security protocols and compliance certifications.

**Step-by-step guide to migrating to a serverless architecture:**

1. **Assess your application:** Identify which parts of your application are suitable for serverless deployment.
2. **Choose a cloud provider:** Select a provider with strong security credentials and serverless capabilities.
3. **Refactor your code:**  Rewrite your application code to function as independent, serverless functions.
4. **Deploy and monitor:** Deploy your serverless functions and closely monitor their performance and security.
