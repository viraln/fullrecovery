## What is Climate Intelligence?

Climate intelligence (CI) is the process of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data to understand and manage climate-related risks and opportunities.  It goes beyond simply acknowledging climate change; it involves proactively assessing its potential impacts on specific assets, operations, and supply chains.  CI is not just about predicting future scenarios; it's about translating complex climate data into actionable insights that inform strategic decision-making.  This involves understanding the physical impacts of climate change (like sea-level rise or extreme weather events) and the resulting transitional impacts (like changes in policy, regulations, or consumer behavior).

> **EXPERT TIP:**  Effective CI isn't a one-time assessment; it's an ongoing process of monitoring, updating, and refining your understanding of climate-related risks.

## Types of Climate Intelligence & Data Sources

CI encompasses various aspects, each requiring different data and analytical approaches:

* **Climate Risk Intelligence:** Focuses on identifying and quantifying the potential financial and operational impacts of climate change on an organization.  This includes physical risks (e.g., flooding, heatwaves) and transitional risks (e.g., carbon pricing, changing regulations).

* **Climate Change Intelligence:**  A broader term encompassing the understanding of the scientific basis of climate change, its projected impacts, and the various mitigation and adaptation strategies.

* **Climate Resilience Intelligence:**  Focuses on building resilience to climate change impacts by identifying vulnerabilities and developing strategies to enhance adaptation and mitigation efforts.
