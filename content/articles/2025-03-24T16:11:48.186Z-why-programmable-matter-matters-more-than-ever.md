## What is Programmable Matter?

Programmable matter refers to materials whose properties, such as shape, stiffness, conductivity, and color, can be altered dynamically and autonomously.  These materials are composed of numerous microscopic or nanoscopic components, often referred to as "programmable atoms" or "programmable particles," that can interact and rearrange themselves according to a pre-programmed set of rules or in response to external stimuli.  This allows for the creation of objects that can change form, adapt to their surroundings, and perform complex tasks without human intervention.  ![Microscopic image of programmable matter particles](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1494192785370-e91e091d544f?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
