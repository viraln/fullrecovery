## What Are Metamaterials? Unveiling the Fundamentals

Metamaterials are artificially engineered materials with properties not found in nature.  Unlike conventional materials whose properties are determined by their chemical composition, metamaterials derive their unique characteristics from their meticulously designed structure, typically at the subwavelength scale.  This means the structural elements are smaller than the wavelength of light they interact with, allowing for unprecedented control over electromagnetic waves, including visible light.
