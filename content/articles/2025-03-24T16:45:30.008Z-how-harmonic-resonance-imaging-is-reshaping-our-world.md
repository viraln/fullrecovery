## What is Harmonic Resonance Imaging?

Harmonic Resonance Imaging is a non-invasive imaging modality that leverages the principles of resonant frequencies to generate high-resolution images of internal structures. Unlike traditional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) which relies on strong magnetic fields, HRI uses targeted, low-energy electromagnetic waves to excite specific atomic nuclei within the body.  These nuclei then emit characteristic signals that are detected and processed to create detailed images. ![A diagram illustrating the basic principles of HRI](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1619691249147-c5689d88016b?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

The beauty of HRI lies in its exceptional sensitivity and specificity. This precision is particularly valuable in oncology, neurology, and cardiology.
