## What is Space Law?  A 2025 Overview

Space law, also known as aerospace law or cosmonautics law, is the body of international and national laws, treaties, and agreements governing activities in outer space. ## Key Space Law Treaties and International Agreements

The Outer Space Treaty of 1967 forms the bedrock of international space law.  It establishes principles such as the freedom of exploration and use of outer space by all states, the prohibition of nuclear weapons in space, and the responsibility of states for national space activities.  However, the treaty is somewhat vague on crucial modern issues.

Other important agreements include the Rescue Agreement (1968), which outlines the responsibilities of states regarding the rescue and return of astronauts, and the Liability Convention (1972), which addresses liability for damage caused by space objects.  The Registration Convention (1976) requires states to register their space objects with the UN.  ![A visual representation of the Outer Space Treaty, perhaps a stylized globe with legal documents orbiting it](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1448932284983-0c7b152eba33?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
