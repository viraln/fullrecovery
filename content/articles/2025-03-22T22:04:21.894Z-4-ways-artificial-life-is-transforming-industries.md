## What is Artificial Life?

Artificial Life, or ALife, is a field of study focused on creating artificial systems that exhibit characteristics of living organisms. This isn't about creating robots that mimic life; instead, it involves designing computational models and simulations that explore the fundamental principles of life, such as adaptation, evolution, and self-organization.  ALife researchers use various techniques, from computer simulations to robotics, to build and study these artificial life forms.  It's a multidisciplinary field drawing from computer science, biology, physics, and mathematics.  Understanding ALife requires grasping its core difference from Artificial Intelligence (AI): while AI focuses on replicating intelligent behavior, ALife aims to replicate the processes and properties of life itself.

> **Did you know?** The term "Artificial Life" was coined by Christopher Langton in 1987, marking the beginning of a dedicated scientific community exploring the principles of life through artificial systems.
