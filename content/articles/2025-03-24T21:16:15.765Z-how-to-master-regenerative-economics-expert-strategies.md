## What is Regenerative Economics?

Regenerative economics moves beyond mere sustainability; it actively seeks to *regenerate* natural and social systems. Unlike traditional economics, which often prioritizes endless growth at the expense of the environment and social equity, regenerative economics aims for a restorative, cyclical economy that benefits all stakeholders.  It emphasizes collaboration, resilience, and the long-term health of both people and the planet.  Think of it as designing an economy that mimics the natural world's regenerative processes, where waste becomes resource and growth is balanced with renewal. ![A vibrant illustration showing a cyclical system representing regenerative economics](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1518302057166-c990a3585cc3?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

## Regenerative Economic Models: Diverse Approaches

Several models exemplify regenerative economic principles:

* **Circular Economy:** Minimizing waste and maximizing resource utilization through reuse, repair, and recycling.
* **Solidarity Economy:** Prioritizing social justice and community empowerment through cooperative ownership and democratic decision-making.
* **Bioregionalism:**  Focusing on local resource management and self-sufficiency within defined geographical areas.
* **Natural Capital Accounting:** Integrating the value of natural resources into economic calculations. ![A chart comparing traditional and regenerative economic models](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1561065465-0928c7ee69cb?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
