## The Rise of AI-Powered Attacks and Defenses

The next decade will be defined by the escalating arms race between AI-powered attackers and AI-powered defenders. > **EXPERT TIP:**  Don't underestimate the power of AI in the hands of malicious actors.  Traditional security measures will become increasingly ineffective without incorporating AI-driven defenses.

## The Expanding Attack Surface: IoT and Edge Computing

The proliferation of Internet of Things (IoT) devices and the rise of edge computing are significantly expanding the attack surface for organizations.  These interconnected devices often lack robust security features, creating numerous entry points for cybercriminals.  ![Graphic showing interconnected IoT devices](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1510915228340-29c85a43dcfe?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **DID YOU KNOW?**  The number of connected IoT devices is projected to exceed 75 billion by 2030, presenting a massive challenge for cybersecurity professionals.

Securing these devices and the data they generate will require a shift in security strategies, focusing on decentralized security architectures and robust authentication protocols.  This includes advancements in embedded security, secure boot processes, and device-level encryption.

##  Quantum Computing: A Double-Edged Sword

Quantum computing, while promising groundbreaking advancements in various fields, also poses a significant threat to current encryption methods.  Quantum computers have the potential to break widely used encryption algorithms, compromising sensitive data and systems.  ![Conceptual image depicting a quantum computer](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1584433144859-1fc3ab64a957?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

The cybersecurity industry is actively working on developing post-quantum cryptography (PQC) algorithms that are resistant to attacks from quantum computers.  The transition to PQC will be a gradual process, requiring careful planning and implementation to avoid disruptions.
