## DNA Origami: The Art of Folding DNA

DNA origami is a particularly fascinating branch of DNA nanotechnology. It involves using a long single-stranded DNA scaffold (often a viral genome) and numerous shorter "staple" strands to fold the scaffold into a desired shape.  The staple strands bind to specific sequences on the scaffold, forcing it to fold and create the predetermined structure.  ![Diagram illustrating the DNA origami process](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1573646609328-01f50a125c0c?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

The versatility of DNA origami is astounding. Scientists have created structures ranging from simple geometric shapes to complex three-dimensional objects, including nanoscale robots capable of performing tasks like targeted drug delivery.
