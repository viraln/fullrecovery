## Understanding Wormholes: A Bridge Through Spacetime

Wormholes, also known as Einstein-Rosen bridges, are hypothetical tunnels connecting two distinct points in spacetime.  This concept arises from Einstein's theory of general relativity, which describes gravity as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy. ![A visual representation of a wormhole connecting two points in spacetime](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1451187580459-43490279c0fa?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)  Imagine spacetime as a fabric; a massive object like a star creates a dip in this fabric. A wormhole, then, is a theoretical shortcut through this fabric, potentially allowing for faster-than-light travel.

> **Did you know?** The theoretical possibility of wormholes was first suggested in 1935 by Albert Einstein and Nathan Rosen, based on solutions to Einstein's field equations.

The mathematics behind wormholes is complex, involving exotic matter with negative mass-energy density. This exotic matter is purely hypothetical and its existence has not been confirmed.  Its properties are crucial for keeping the wormhole open and stable, preventing its immediate collapse.
