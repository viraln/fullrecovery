## What are Wormholes, and Where Did the Idea Come From?

Wormholes, also known as Einstein-Rosen bridges, are hypothetical tunnels through spacetime.  This mind-bending concept arises from Einstein's theory of general relativity, which describes gravity as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy.  ![Artistic depiction of a wormhole connecting two points in spacetime](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1723542922013-10e3efa44275?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)  Mathematically, Einstein's equations allow for the existence of these bridges, connecting two distant points in space or even different points in time. > **DID YOU KNOW?** The term "wormhole" wasn't coined by Einstein.  It was introduced much later by physicist John Wheeler, who likened these tunnels to a worm burrowing through an apple, creating a shortcut from one side to the other.
