Infinite scaling, in its purest sense, refers to the ability of a system to seamlessly handle an unlimited number of users, requests, or data points without performance degradation. Think of it as a living organism, constantly adjusting its form and function to maintain optimal performance.  This differs from traditional scaling methods, which often hit limitations as growth accelerates.

> **EXPERT TIP:**  True infinite scaling is a theoretical ideal.  In practice, we aim for *near-infinite* scalability, meaning the system can adapt to virtually any foreseeable demand within practical constraints like budget and available technology.
