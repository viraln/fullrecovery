## Understanding Biosphere Design: A Deep Dive into Closed Ecological Systems

Biosphere design, at its core, is the art and science of creating self-contained, artificial ecosystems. These "closed ecological systems" mimic the natural processes found in Earth's biosphere, but on a smaller, controlled scale.  The goal is to create a stable, sustainable environment capable of supporting life, often including plants, animals, and microorganisms, with minimal external input.  This contrasts sharply with open systems, which rely heavily on external resources and waste disposal.  Think of a terrarium as a rudimentary example, while Biosphere 2 represents a significantly more ambitious attempt. ![A diagram illustrating the key components of a closed ecological system](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1506620101082-6d82db519896?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **DID YOU KNOW?**  The concept of closed ecological systems isn't new.  Early experiments with sealed terrariums date back centuries, laying the groundwork for the more complex biosphere designs we see today.

## Biosphere 2: Lessons Learned from a Groundbreaking Experiment
