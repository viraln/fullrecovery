## What Exactly is a Living Building?

The term "living building" refers to structures that go far beyond traditional green building practices. They aim to function like natural ecosystems, generating their own energy, capturing and treating water, and operating in harmony with their surroundings. The Living Building Challenge (LBC), a rigorous green building certification program, provides a framework for achieving this ambitious goal.

*   **Beautiful:** Inspiring and aesthetically pleasing.

## Myth 1: Living Buildings are Only for Rural Areas

One common misconception is that living buildings are only feasible in remote, rural settings with ample space and natural resources. While rural environments can offer advantages, living building principles can be adapted to urban contexts as well.

## Living Building Materials: A Critical Component

The materials used in a living building are just as important as its energy and water systems. Living buildings prioritize the use of materials that are:

*   **Locally sourced:** Reducing transportation emissions and supporting local economies.
*   **Renewable:** Made from resources that can be replenished naturally.
*   **Non-toxic:** Free from harmful chemicals that can impact human health and the environment.
*   **Reclaimed or recycled:** Diverting waste from landfills and conserving resources.

> **EXPERT TIP:** The Living Building Challenge has a "Red List" of materials that are prohibited due to their environmental or health impacts. Be sure to avoid these materials when selecting building products.

The benefits of living buildings extend far beyond environmental sustainability. They can also have a positive impact on human health, well-being, and the economy.
