## What is Digital Physics?

Digital physics, at its core, postulates that the universe, or at least a significant portion of it, is fundamentally discrete and informational, much like a computer simulation. Instead of continuous spacetime and fields, it suggests that reality is built from discrete units of information, processed according to a set of underlying rules. This "digital code" would underpin everything from the smallest subatomic particles to the largest cosmic structures.  ![A stylized representation of a digital universe](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1451187580459-43490279c0fa?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **EXPERT TIP:**  Don't confuse digital physics with simply using computers to *simulate* physics. Digital physics proposes that the simulation *is* reality, or at least a very close approximation.

Several models exist within digital physics, ranging from cellular automata (like Conway's Game of Life) to more sophisticated approaches involving quantum computation and information theory. These models attempt to explain fundamental physical phenomena, such as gravity, quantum entanglement, and the arrow of time, as emergent properties of this underlying digital substrate.
