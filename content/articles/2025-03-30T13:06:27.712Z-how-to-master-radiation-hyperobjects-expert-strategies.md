## Current Strategies for Managing Radiation Hyperobjects

Dealing with radioactive waste is a global challenge, and various strategies are being employed (with varying degrees of success).

1.  **Geological Repositories:** The most widely accepted solution involves burying radioactive waste deep underground in stable geological formations. The idea is to isolate the waste from the biosphere for tens of thousands of years. Examples include the proposed Yucca Mountain repository in the US (currently stalled) and the Onkalo spent nuclear fuel repository in Finland.

2.  **Interim Storage:** Many countries rely on interim storage facilities, such as dry cask storage, to temporarily house nuclear waste. However, these facilities are not designed for long-term storage and pose a risk of leakage or accidents.

3.  **Reprocessing:** Some countries, like France, reprocess spent nuclear fuel to extract usable materials like uranium and plutonium. This reduces the volume of high-level waste but also creates new waste streams and raises proliferation concerns.

4.  **Advanced Reactor Technologies:** Research is underway on advanced reactor designs that could potentially consume existing nuclear waste or produce less hazardous waste products. However, these technologies are still in the early stages of development.
