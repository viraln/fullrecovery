## How Does Molecular Storage Work?  Unlocking the Potential of DNA

Molecular digital storage, primarily utilizing DNA storage, leverages the incredible information density of DNA molecules.  Think of it as a biological hard drive.  Digital data is translated into a sequence of DNA bases (A, T, C, G), which are then synthesized and stored physically.  To retrieve the data, the DNA is sequenced, and the sequence is translated back into digital information. ![Microscopic image of DNA strands](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1526374965328-7f61d4dc18c5?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **DID YOU KNOW?** A single gram of DNA can theoretically store the equivalent of all the data in the world today?

This process involves several key steps:

1. **Data Encoding:**  The digital data is converted into a binary code, then translated into a DNA base sequence.
2. **DNA Synthesis:**  Specialized machines synthesize the DNA strands based on the generated sequence.
3. **Storage:** The synthesized DNA is stored in a stable, dry environment, often using specialized containers.
4. **Data Retrieval:** When needed, the DNA is sequenced, and the sequence is translated back into digital data.
5. **Data Decoding:** The retrieved binary code is converted back into its original digital format.
