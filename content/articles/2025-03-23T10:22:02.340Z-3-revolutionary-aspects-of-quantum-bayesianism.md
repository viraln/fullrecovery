## 1. Subjectivity of Quantum States: Your Beliefs, Your Reality

Unlike traditional interpretations of quantum mechanics, QBism argues that quantum states are not objective descriptions of reality. Instead, they represent an agent's personal degree of belief about the outcomes of future quantum measurements.  Think of it like this: a quantum state isn't a property *of* the system, but rather a *description of your knowledge* about the system. ![A stylized graphic depicting a person observing a quantum system, with probability clouds surrounding the system.](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1651955784685-f969100bfc25?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

This subjective interpretation completely changes the game.
