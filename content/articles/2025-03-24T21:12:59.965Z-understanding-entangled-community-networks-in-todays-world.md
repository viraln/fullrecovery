## What are Entangled Community Networks?

Entangled community networks, also known as entangled networks, represent a paradigm shift in internet infrastructure. Unlike centralized models dominated by large corporations, these networks are built on a mesh topology, utilizing multiple interconnected nodes to create a resilient and self-healing system.  This decentralized approach empowers communities to own and control their internet access, fostering digital sovereignty and reducing reliance on external providers.  Think of it as a web, rather than a single, easily breakable thread. ![Illustration of a mesh network compared to a centralized network](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1522543558187-768b6df7c25c?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

Key characteristics of entangled community networks include:

* 🔑 **Decentralization:** No single point of failure. If one node goes down, the network remains operational.
* ⚡ **Resilience:**  Resistant to censorship and external attacks.
* ✅ **Community Ownership:**  Operated and maintained by the community itself, promoting local control and digital equity.
* 🌐 **Open Source:** Often built using open-source hardware and software, fostering collaboration and innovation.

## The Technology Behind Entangled Networks

Entangled network technology leverages various protocols and technologies, primarily mesh networking.  Mesh networks allow devices to communicate directly with each other, creating a self-organizing network without the need for a central server or router.  This eliminates single points of failure and increases network robustness.  Common technologies used include:

* **Wireless Mesh Protocols:**  These protocols govern how devices communicate within the mesh network, ensuring efficient data transmission and network stability. Examples include OLSR, B.A.T.M.A.N. advanced, and others.
* **Open Source Software:**  Software like FreeRADIUS and other open-source tools are crucial for managing user authentication, access control, and network security.
* **Hardware:** A variety of routers, antennas, and other equipment can be used to build a mesh network, ranging from off-the-shelf consumer devices to purpose-built, high-performance options.

> **EXPERT TIP:** Choosing the right hardware and software is crucial for the success of your entangled community network. Consider factors like range, security, and ease of maintenance when making your selections.
