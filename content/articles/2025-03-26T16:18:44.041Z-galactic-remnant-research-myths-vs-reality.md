## Galactic Remnant Formation: A Violent Cosmic Ballet

Galactic remnants are formed through several dramatic processes:

1. **Galactic Collisions:** The most spectacular formation mechanism involves the collision of two or more galaxies.  This can lead to a complete merger, resulting in a single, larger galaxy, or a partial disruption, leaving behind a complex remnant.
2. **Tidal Stripping:** When a smaller galaxy passes close to a larger one, the gravitational forces of the larger galaxy can strip away stars and gas from the smaller one, creating a stream or tail of galactic material â€“ a type of remnant.
3. **Ram Pressure Stripping:** As galaxies move through clusters of galaxies, the hot intergalactic medium can strip away gas from the galaxy's outer regions. This process leaves behind a stripped galaxy, often considered a remnant.
