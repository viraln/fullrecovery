##  The Fundamentals of Wave Interference

Wave interference occurs when two or more waves overlap in space. The resulting pattern depends on the properties of the individual waves, primarily their amplitude (height) and phase (position within their cycle).  When waves are in phase (crests align with crests, troughs with troughs), they undergo **constructive interference**, resulting in a wave with a larger amplitude. Conversely, **destructive interference** happens when waves are out of phase (crests align with troughs), leading to a wave with a smaller amplitude, or even cancellation. ![Illustration of constructive and destructive interference](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1501436513145-30f24e19fcc8?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)

> **EXPERT TIP:**  Understanding the phase relationship between waves is crucial for predicting the outcome of interference.

This fundamental principle governs various wave phenomena, including sound, light, water waves, and even matter waves (as described by quantum mechanics).
