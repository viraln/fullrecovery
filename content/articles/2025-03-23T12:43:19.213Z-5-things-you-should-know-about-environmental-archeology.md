## 3.  Climate Change Archaeology: Lessons from the Past

**Climate change archaeology** is a rapidly growing subfield that explores the impacts of past climate change on human societies. By studying past climate events and their effects on human populations, we can gain valuable insights into how societies adapted to, or were overwhelmed by, environmental changes.  This knowledge is crucial for understanding the potential impacts of current climate change and developing effective adaptation strategies.  For instance, studies of the collapse of the Maya civilization highlight the potential consequences of prolonged drought and resource scarcity.
