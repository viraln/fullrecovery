## 2. Key Concepts: Superposition, Entanglement, and Measurement in Social Systems

Several core quantum concepts find parallels in social dynamics:

* **Superposition:** An individual might simultaneously hold conflicting beliefs or opinions (e.g., supporting environmental protection while also consuming environmentally damaging products). This state of superposition resolves only upon "measurement" (e.g., making a purchase decision).
* **Entanglement:**  The actions or beliefs of one individual can instantaneously affect others within their social network, even if geographically distant. This entanglement explains the rapid spread of trends, opinions, and even social unrest.
* **Measurement:** The act of observing or measuring a social system (e.g., conducting a survey) can alter the system itself, introducing bias and affecting future outcomes. ![A network graph illustrating entanglement between individuals](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1484417894907-623942c8ee29?q=85&w=1200&fit=max&fm=webp&auto=compress)
